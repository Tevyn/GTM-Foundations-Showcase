# Sortly -- Ideal Customer Profile

## Target Market
**Industries:** Construction, medical/healthcare, warehousing & logistics, IT/facilities management, electrical, HVAC, plumbing, retail, education, events, interior design, government, non-profit, dental, manufacturing, real estate, restaurant
**Company Size:** 1-500+ employees; sole proprietors through mid-market organizations
**Geography:** Primarily North America; available globally (English-language product)

## Technographics
Currently tracking inventory using spreadsheets (Excel, Google Sheets), pen-and-paper systems, or a basic inventory tool (InFlow, Inventory Now, etc.). May use QuickBooks for accounting. Some teams use Slack or Microsoft Teams for internal communication. Mobile device usage is high -- field teams rely on phones and tablets for day-to-day work.

## Buying Triggers
- Looking to move off spreadsheets or manual tracking processes
- Need better visibility into inventory across locations
- Want to reduce time spent on inventory counts and audits
- Growing team needs shared access to inventory data
- Looking to improve accuracy and reduce losses

## Buyer Roles
Business owners, office managers, operations managers, IT managers, facilities managers, warehouse managers, and team leads across a wide range of departments and industries. In smaller organizations the buyer is often the owner or founder; in mid-market companies it may be a department head or operations director.

## Pain Points
- Manual tracking is time-consuming and error-prone
- Teams struggle to find or locate items quickly
- No real-time visibility into what's in stock across multiple locations
- Spreadsheets break down as the team or inventory grows
- Difficulty managing check-in/check-out for shared tools and equipment
- Lack of alerts for low stock or expiring items
- Hard to generate reports or prove accountability for audits
- Field teams can't access inventory data when away from the office

## Not a Fit
- Large enterprises requiring deep ERP integration or complex manufacturing workflows (MRP, BOM management)
- Companies needing regulatory compliance tracking beyond what Sortly supports (e.g., pharmaceutical serialization, FDA-regulated inventory)
- Businesses that require advanced warehouse management features like wave picking, zone management, or automated conveyor integration
