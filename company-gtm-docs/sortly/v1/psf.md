# Sortly -- Problem-Solution Overview

## Business Owner / Executive Perspective
**Priorities:** Reducing operational costs, minimizing inventory shrinkage and losses, improving accountability across teams
**How Sortly Helps:** Sortly provides a centralized inventory management platform that gives leadership real-time visibility into assets and supplies across the organization, helping reduce waste and improve decision-making.

## Operations / Warehouse Manager Perspective
**Priorities:** Accurate inventory counts, efficient workflows for receiving and distributing items, team coordination across locations
**How Sortly Helps:** Sortly streamlines day-to-day inventory operations with barcode/QR scanning, bulk import, and folder-based organization so managers can keep things running smoothly without manual data entry bottlenecks.

## Field Team / End User Perspective
**Priorities:** Quick access to inventory information on the go, easy check-in/check-out for tools and equipment, not having to call the office to check stock levels
**How Sortly Helps:** Sortly's mobile app with offline access lets field workers look up items, scan barcodes, and update inventory from job sites without needing a desktop or internet connection.

## IT / Facilities Stakeholder
**Priorities:** Asset tracking and lifecycle management, integration with existing tools, minimal IT overhead for deployment
**How Sortly Helps:** Sortly integrates with QuickBooks, Slack, and Microsoft Teams, and offers API/webhooks for custom workflows, with minimal setup required since it's cloud-based with no on-premise infrastructure.

## Use Cases

### Visual Inventory Tracking Across Locations
**Pain:** Teams managing inventory across multiple sites -- warehouses, offices, job sites, vehicles -- rely on spreadsheets or paper lists that go stale the moment someone moves, uses, or restocks an item. Managers spend hours each week reconciling counts and chasing down discrepancies. Photos help field workers identify the right item when part numbers or descriptions are ambiguous.
**Solution:** Sortly's photo-based item entries, QR/barcode scanning, and real-time cloud sync let teams document and track every item visually. Folder organization and custom fields keep things structured, while the mobile app ensures updates happen at the point of activity -- not hours later at a desk.

### Tool and Equipment Check-In/Check-Out
**Pain:** Shared tools and equipment go missing or sit idle because there's no system for tracking who has what. Teams resort to sign-out sheets or group texts.
**Solution:** Sortly's check-in/check-out feature with user tracking and activity history. Teams can see which items are out, who has them, and when they're expected back.

### Low Stock and Expiration Alerts
**Pain:** Running out of critical supplies or discovering expired materials causes project delays and wasted spend. Most teams only find out about shortages during a manual count.
**Solution:** Sortly's low stock alerts and date-based alerts notify the right people automatically, enabling proactive reordering and reducing waste from expired inventory.
