Congrats on the round. This is the stage where most platform leads I talk to hit a wall with their monitoring stack — you've got Datadog, CloudWatch, and probably Prometheus all firing alerts independently, and your on-call team can't tell what's real anymore.

The typical move is to consolidate everything into Datadog. But when the same database failure is triggering alerts in three tools simultaneously, consolidation doesn't fix the noise — it just gives you one UI to be overwhelmed in. The real problem is the overlap between tools, and no single tool can see that.

A platform lead at a similar-stage SaaS company was dealing with **94 alerts a day**. Within three weeks they were down to **28** actionable signals — MTTR dropped from 18 minutes to 6, and they haven't missed a critical incident since.
