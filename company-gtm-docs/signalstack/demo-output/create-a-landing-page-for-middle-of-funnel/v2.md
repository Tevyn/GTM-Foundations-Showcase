## Your monitoring stack was built to catch problems. Now it's the problem.

You added Datadog for APM. CloudWatch for infrastructure. Prometheus for Kubernetes. Each tool solved a real problem when you brought it in. But now they fire independently — the same failure triggers alerts in three places at once, and your on-call engineer has no idea which one is the root cause and which are symptoms.

Your team gets **50-100+ alerts per day**. They've stopped trusting their own pagers. Last month, a critical database incident was buried in 40 duplicates and 30 false positives. By the time someone noticed, the customer had been down for 35 minutes. SLA breach. Angry account team. Renewal at risk.

Meanwhile, your platform engineers — the ones you hired to build infrastructure — are spending a quarter of their time maintaining alert rules across three tools. And your best SREs are updating their resumes because on-call has become 80% noise.

## How engineering teams reclaim signal across their full stack

**From 100 alerts to 20 real incidents — across every tool you use**

SignalStack sits between your monitoring tools and PagerDuty. It sees across your entire stack — not just within one tool — and identifies which alerts are genuinely correlated to real incidents. Your Datadog CPU spike and your CloudWatch RDS latency alarm are the same event. SignalStack knows that. Your current tools don't.

**On-call pages worth waking up for**

Noise gets filtered before it reaches your incident management platform. Your on-call engineer gets paged for real incidents, not duplicates and false positives. Pages drop from 18/week to 6/week. The engineers who were considering leaving decide to stay — because on-call is manageable again.

**Platform team builds infrastructure, not alert rules**

Automated rule governance replaces the manual treadmill of maintaining configurations across Datadog, Prometheus, and CloudWatch. Your platform team recovers 15-20 hours per week — enough to ship the infrastructure projects that have been sitting on the backlog for six months.
