When a genuine perimeter breach fires at 2 AM, it looks identical to the 200 false alarms before it. Meanwhile, every new facility means another $800K in guard costs, and a single incident investigation consumes two days of manual video scrubbing across dozens of cameras.

**Challenge:** Top of Funnel Growth, Engagement & Conversion

**V1:** The original ICP spans eight industries and six buyer roles, so the one-pager opens with a product description that could apply to any company in any segment.

**V2:** The focused ICP targets one buyer type in one industry, so the one-pager opens with a specific operational reality the reader recognizes from their own facility.

**Impact:** Content that leads with the product instead of the buyer's world gets filtered as vendor noise — content that describes a problem the reader is living earns consideration and pipeline.

# Reveal

## Top of Funnel Growth

**ICP:** Most companies define their ICP as an industry list and a company size range — so every piece of content has to be written for everyone and ends up resonating with no one. When ICP work identifies a specific buyer, their triggers, and their operational reality, every asset can open with something that audience recognizes — and that relevance is what focuses budget and creates pipeline.

**Problem-solution:** Most companies describe their problem at headline level — "reduce false alarms" or "improve response times" — so their content sounds interchangeable. When problem-solution work maps the specific mechanism that burns time and money, content can describe the buyer's real situation — and that specificity is what earns attention and drives demand.

## Engagement & Conversion

**ICP:** Most companies write for a broad audience, so their content reads as product-first and requires the buyer to figure out if it applies to them. When ICP work targets a specific buyer with specific priorities, content can speak directly to that person's world — and that relevance is what drives engagement and conversion.

**Problem-solution:** Most companies list capabilities without grounding them in the buyer's workflow, so proof points float without context. When problem-solution work maps specific before-and-after outcomes, proof becomes a story the reader sees themselves in — and that specificity is what moves buyers from browsing to evaluating.

**Messaging:** Most companies organize content around product features, so every section introduces a new concept with no connecting thread. When messaging is organized around a single idea, every section reinforces the same story — and that consistency is what compounds into trust and higher conversion.

## Retention

**Messaging:** Most companies have different value stories across their website, sales deck, and collateral — so buyers hear three different products. When messaging is consistent and proof reflects the buyer's actual experience, expectations set during the sale match what customers see after — and that alignment is what reduces churn and drives referrals.
