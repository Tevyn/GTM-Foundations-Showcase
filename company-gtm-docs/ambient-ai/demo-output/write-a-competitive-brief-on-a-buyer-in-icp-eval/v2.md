# Competitive Brief: Verkada in Consolidated Precision Manufacturing Deal

## Deal Context

Consolidated Precision Manufacturing (CPM) is a **$2.1B aerospace parts manufacturer** with four facilities across the Midwest, running **620 cameras on Genetec VMS** with Honeywell access control and a 36-person guard force. Their VP of Global Security, **Mark Ellison**, is evaluating both Ambient.ai and Verkada after a perimeter breach at their Toledo facility in Q4 resulted in $180K in raw materials theft and an insurance investigation that took 11 days to close. Verkada entered the deal through CPM's VP of IT, who saw a demo at ISC West and was drawn to the modern cloud dashboard and simplified camera management pitch. Ellison is running parallel evaluations and expects to make a decision within 90 days.

---

## Where We Win Against Verkada

- **CPM has 620 cameras already deployed.** Verkada's model requires replacing every camera with their proprietary hardware. At CPM's scale, that's a **$1.5M-$3M+ capital project** before any intelligence is delivered — plus months of installation downtime across four manufacturing facilities where production can't stop. Ambient.ai layers onto the existing Genetec/Honeywell infrastructure on day one. This isn't a marginal advantage; it's the difference between a 60-day deployment and a 12-18 month infrastructure overhaul.

- **Alarm fatigue is CPM's primary operational pain.** Ellison's GSOC team processes **500+ alerts/day** across four sites with a self-reported false alarm rate above 90%. Verkada's analytics are basic motion detection and people counting — the same rule-based approach that's generating the noise CPM is drowning in today. Ambient.ai's Access Intelligence correlates badge events with video context, reducing false alarms by **95%** — operators go from dismissing 450 false alerts to reviewing 25-40 verified events worth their attention.

- **The investigation bottleneck is what opened this deal.** The Toledo breach took 11 days to produce evidence for the insurance claim because analysts were manually scrubbing footage across 40+ cameras. Verkada's search is limited to their own cameras — it can't search across CPM's existing Genetec-managed feeds. Ambient.ai's Advanced Forensics searches across every camera on the network by content — describe what happened and the system surfaces relevant footage. That 11-day investigation becomes a **45-minute forensic review**.

- **Guard optimization is the CFO's priority.** CPM spends an estimated **$2.3M/year** on their 36-guard force. Expanding to a fifth facility (planned for next fiscal year) means another $800K+ in guard costs. Ambient.ai's continuous AI monitoring and Threat Detection convert guard deployment from fixed patrols to intelligence-directed response — a **20-30% efficiency gain** that flattens the cost curve for every facility CPM adds. Verkada doesn't address guard optimization because their model is camera-centric, not operations-centric.

---

## Where Verkada Wins

- **Greenfield deployments.** If CPM were building a brand-new facility with no existing cameras, Verkada's integrated camera-plus-cloud model would be simpler to deploy from scratch. Their hardware is modern, the UX is clean, and there's no integration work. But that's not CPM's situation — they're adding intelligence to 620 cameras they've already paid for, not starting over.

- **IT buyer preference for simplicity.** Verkada's pitch resonates with IT leaders who want a single-vendor cloud dashboard. CPM's VP of IT introduced Verkada for this reason. But the actual security operations — alarm management, guard deployment, investigations — are Ellison's domain, and his problems require intelligence that Verkada's platform doesn't deliver at CPM's scale and complexity.

---

## The Value Wedge

CPM's security infrastructure represents **years of investment** — 620 IP cameras, Genetec VMS, Honeywell access control, a 36-person guard force. The problem isn't that the infrastructure is wrong. The problem is that nothing connects it intelligently. Cameras record but don't understand. Access control logs but doesn't correlate. Guards walk perimeters whether or not anything is happening.

Verkada's answer is to **throw it all away and start over** with their cameras. Ambient.ai's answer is to make everything CPM already has **actually work together** — adding the intelligence layer that verifies alerts before they reach operators, directs guards to verified threats, and searches footage by what happened instead of by timestamp.

Verkada structurally cannot follow us here. Their business model depends on selling their own camera hardware. They can't be the intelligence layer on someone else's cameras — that's fundamentally not what they do. For a manufacturer with CPM's existing infrastructure, this is the conversation-ending differentiator.

## Talk Track: When Ellison Brings Up Verkada

*Use this conversationally — peer-level, not defensive. The goal is to reframe the evaluation criteria around CPM's actual operational problems, not camera features.*

"Mark, I've seen a few evaluations where Verkada comes in through the IT side — the dashboard is slick and the pitch is clean. The question I'd put in front of you is: what problem are you actually solving?

If the problem is camera management, Verkada's a reasonable option — but you'd be ripping out 620 cameras and your entire Genetec deployment to get there. That's a multi-million dollar infrastructure project before you've addressed a single false alarm or shaved a single day off your investigation timeline.

From what you've shared, the real problems are your operators drowning in 500 alerts a day, your team spending almost two weeks to close out the Toledo incident, and your guard costs growing every time you add a facility. Those are intelligence problems, not camera problems. And they're problems we solve on the infrastructure you already have — no rip-and-replace.

I'd actually suggest this: run our pilot on one facility alongside your Verkada evaluation. Within 60 days, measure false alarm reduction on your existing cameras. If we don't hit 90%+ reduction, you'll know. But I think what you'll see is that your cameras were never the problem — the intelligence connecting them was."
