# GTM Foundations — Shared Context

## Company Overview
LaunchPad is a campaign orchestration platform for marketing teams managing multiple tools. It sits above the existing martech stack to unify campaign execution, reporting, and budget management across fragmented tools like Marketo, HubSpot, Salesforce, and ad platforms.

## ICP Context (added by ICP skill)

### Pain Patterns (for PSF to quantify and map to solutions)
- **Pain areas:** Campaign coordination complexity, fragmented reporting, manual UTM/budget management, ROI visibility gaps
- **Buyer motivations:** Improved team efficiency, faster campaign execution, clearer performance visibility
- **Industries showing strongest adoption:** Tech (product launches, customer campaigns), Financial Services (compliance-heavy campaign coordination)
- **Key qualification signals:** Number of active campaigns, count of marketing tools in stack, reporting frequency/time spent

### Competitive & Market Context (for Positioning)
- **Primary competitors:** HubSpot built-in automation, Zapier/Zapier-like DIY integrations, Monday.com for marketing ops teams
- **Key differentiator themes:** Purpose-built for campaign orchestration vs. general automation platforms; unified analytics across all tools vs. native single-tool reports
- **Win signals:** Prospects frustrated with Excel-driven ROI tracking, managing 5+ integrated marketing tools, struggling with UTM consistency
- **Adjacent categories:** Marketing operations platforms, marketing automation tools, analytics consolidation solutions

### Buyer Language & Framing (for Messaging)
- **Buyer language:** "Campaign chaos," "reporting nightmare," "tool sprawl," "can't prove campaign ROI," "manual UTM management is a bottleneck"
- **Content themes:** How enterprises manage multi-tool stacks, ROI reporting challenges, campaign coordination best practices, marketing operations scaling
- **Buyer motivation framing:** LaunchPad positions orchestration as the control layer for fragmented marketing ops — not replacing tools but unifying them
- **Tone signals:** Professional, operations-focused; speaks to marketing leaders frustrated with tool complexity

## PSF Context (added by PSF skill)

### Problem-Solution Observations
- **Flagship strength:** Campaign orchestration and unified reporting — the core pain (fragmented performance data) maps clearly to LaunchPad's core capability (cross-platform orchestration and analytics). This is the use case the sales team will lead with because it touches the broadest buyer motivation: proving ROI and reducing reporting overhead.
- **Secondary capabilities:** UTM management, budget allocation, campaign scaling — all tied to operational efficiency and reducing manual work. These have product support but less sales maturity; they address narrower pain points (consistency, speed) rather than the headline "I can't measure campaign ROI" problem.
- **Buyer language alignment:** "Campaign chaos," "reporting nightmare," and "can't prove ROI" directly map to the flagship use case. Secondary use cases align with operational frustration but less with strategic urgency.
- **Key qualification signals for sales:** Number of active campaigns (volume problem), count of marketing tools in stack (integration problem), reporting frequency and time spent (efficiency problem). The bigger these numbers, the clearer the flagship use case resonates.

## Positioning Observations (added by Positioning skill)
- **Primary competitors:** HubSpot built-in automation (ecosystem-locked), Zapier/DIY integrations (workflow triggers without analytics), Monday.com marketing (project tracking, not execution), manual spreadsheets (default for reporting)
- **Win pattern:** Marketing teams running 5+ tools with fragmented reporting — the "Friday afternoon reporting nightmare" is the entry point
- **Lose pattern:** HubSpot-only or Marketo-only shops without integration pain; teams without dedicated marketing ops to drive adoption
- **Rough positioning claim:** "Campaign orchestration platform" — unifies execution, reporting, and budget management across fragmented martech stack
- **Perceived differentiators:** Cross-platform orchestration (strongest), unified analytics, UTM management, works with existing tools rather than replacing them
- **Market category assumption:** Marketing operations / campaign orchestration — sits between marketing automation (Marketo/HubSpot) and project management (Monday/Asana), distinct from both
- **Positioning gap:** No clear value wedge — differentiators are listed as capabilities rather than anchored in a specific buyer's decision criteria or competitive frame

## Messaging Observations (added by Messaging skill)
- **Elevator pitch:** "LaunchPad is a campaign orchestration platform that unifies your marketing tools into one command center"
- **Lead value prop:** One Command Center (strongest, most polished — "Orchestrate campaigns across every marketing tool from a single platform")
- **Secondary value props:** Unified campaign ROI analytics, faster campaign launches via automated setup
- **Brand voice adjectives:** Efficient, Clear, Reliable
- **Messaging pattern:** Feature-framed value props describing platform capabilities; no narrative arc; no buyer language integration; marketing-audience only
