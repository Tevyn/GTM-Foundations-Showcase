# LaunchPad — Positioning

## Our Vision
We believe marketing teams deserve to spend their time on strategy, not on wrestling with tools. LaunchPad exists to simplify campaign management by bringing together the fragmented martech stack into one orchestration layer that gives teams control, visibility, and speed.

## What We Do
LaunchPad is a campaign orchestration platform that sits above your existing marketing tools — Marketo, HubSpot, Salesforce, ad platforms — to unify campaign execution, reporting, and budget management. It eliminates the manual coordination between platforms so marketing teams can launch, track, and optimize campaigns from a single command center.

| Budget allocation tracking | ✓ | Basic | ✗ | Partial | Manual / spreadsheet |
| Cross-channel attribution | ✓ | Limited | ✗ | ✗ | Manual |

Unlike HubSpot's built-in automation, LaunchPad works across your entire martech stack — not just one ecosystem. Unlike Zapier integrations, LaunchPad provides analytics and orchestration, not just workflow triggers. Unlike Monday.com, LaunchPad executes campaigns across platforms rather than just tracking project status.

## Key Differentiators
- **Purpose-built for campaign orchestration:** Not a general automation tool or project management platform — designed specifically for marketing teams running campaigns across multiple tools
- **Unified analytics across all platforms:** See campaign performance from every tool in one dashboard instead of pulling reports from 5+ platforms into spreadsheets
- **Works with your existing stack:** Sits on top of Marketo, HubSpot, Salesforce, and ad platforms without replacing them — protects existing tool investments
- **Faster campaign deployment:** Launch and coordinate campaigns across multiple platforms from one interface instead of logging into each tool separately

## Positioning Statement
"LaunchPad is a campaign orchestration platform that helps marketing teams manage, measure, and optimize campaigns across their entire martech stack — from one unified command center."
