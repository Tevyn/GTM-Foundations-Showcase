# LaunchPad — Problem-Solution Fit

## Overview
This document maps four critical problems facing enterprise SaaS marketing teams running $50M-$500M ARR and orchestrating 12+ concurrent campaigns annually. Each problem connects directly to the ICP's economic buyer priorities: pipeline attribution, team velocity, and proof of ROI to finance. LaunchPad's value is highest for companies already locked into Marketo/Salesforce/LinkedIn multi-tool stacks—where fragmentation is most acute and the cost of orchestration breakdown is highest. This PSF prioritizes problems where LaunchPad has differentiated solution capability and where pain translates directly to revenue or cost outcomes the economic buyer (VP of Marketing + CFO/finance stakeholder) can quantify.

## Problem Areas

### 1. Campaign Coordination Breaks Under Volume—Causing Wasted Spend and Attribution Chaos

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Pain (Specific)** | Across 3-5 concurrent campaigns (product launch, seasonal demand, event nurture), marketing ops discovers overlapping audiences, duplicate email touches, and inconsistent UTM codes only after campaigns are live—sometimes weeks in. A single ops person cannot manage audience sync from Marketo to LinkedIn, validate UTMs across four ad platforms, and coordinate landing page copy changes without one task bleeding into the next. Friday afternoon: ops person manually counts unique contacts in Marketo to reconcile against LinkedIn spend. Result: campaigns run with broken UTMs, audiences overlap, paid spend targets the wrong audience segment, and no one can answer "did this campaign work?" Two ops people can't fix it without hiring headcount; scaling to 15+ campaigns requires either a third FTE or a tool. The frustration is operational (constant firefighting) and financial—the VP of Marketing can't tell the CFO whether the $2M annual ad budget drove the claimed pipeline. |
| **Impact (Quantified)** | At the ICP scale ($50M-$500M ARR, 200-2,000 employees), assume: (1) Current state: 1.5 FTE Marketing Ops at $135K loaded cost = $202.5K annually; 30% of ops time consumed by manual coordination (UTM building, audience sync, campaign-to-Salesforce validation) = $60K/year in wasted ops capacity. (2) Mistargeted spend due to audience overlap and incorrect UTMs: if 20% of $2M annual paid budget ($400K) is misdirected to low-performing audiences or duplicated contacts due to coordination failures, that's $400K in wasted spend annually. (3) Opportunity cost of delayed campaigns: assume 2-3 major campaigns per year experience 1-2 week delays due to ops bottleneck (product launches, seasonal demand peaks). If each campaign drives $500K-$1M in pipeline and delay costs 10% of that window, that's $100K-$300K per incident × 2-3 incidents = $200K-$900K in lost opportunity per year. **Total economic impact: $660K-$1.26M annually from wasted ops time + misdirected spend + delayed campaigns.** |
| **Solution (Mapped)** | LaunchPad resolves campaign coordination chaos by consolidating the orchestration workflow into a single source of truth for campaign setup, audience sync, and UTM management. Specifically: (1) Campaign templates eliminate the need to rebuild audience rules, UTMs, and sync workflows from scratch; (2) Pre-built Marketo-to-Salesforce-to-LinkedIn sync automations replace manual verification steps—ops team confirms audience logic once, LaunchPad handles the ongoing sync; (3) Unified UTM builder ensures consistent naming across all campaigns and platforms; (4) Campaign orchestration timeline view shows which tasks are blocking (audience not synced, landing page pending) vs. ready to launch, eliminating the Friday-afternoon discovery of misconfigurations. The approach addresses root cause (lack of coordination visibility and repeatable orchestration processes) rather than symptom (hiring more ops). Ops team moves from firefighting to managing campaigns at scale. |

**Objections:**
- "Can't we just build this in Zapier or Integromat?" → Underlying concern: LaunchPad looks like a "nice to have" orchestration layer when DIY automation is free. Response: Zapier handles one-off workflows; campaign orchestration at scale requires pre-built audience sync logic, UTM validation, and budget-aware sync (prevents over-spends). Zapier workflows break when Marketo schema changes; LaunchPad updates automatically. DIY also requires an engineer's time to maintain; the hidden cost of Zapier for orchestration (30-40 hours/quarter to maintain/debug) exceeds LaunchPad's cost by Q2. Real-world comparison: [Customer in ICP segment] spent 15 hours/week debugging Zapier integrations; switched to LaunchPad and reduced to 2 hours/week managing orchestration.

- "Our Marketo/Salesforce native orchestration should handle this." → Underlying concern: existing platform native features seem to overlap; vendor wants to stay in platform. Response: Marketo Journey Orchestrator and Salesforce Flow excel at execution within single systems; they cannot see audience state across Salesforce + LinkedIn + Google Ads + Marketo simultaneously, cannot validate UTM consistency across paid and owned channels, and cannot enforce budget guardrails that span ad platforms. LaunchPad's value is the *above-stack* visibility that prevents coordination failures. [Customer in ICP segment] invested 8 weeks building native Journey Orchestrator workflows; abandoned the POC when they realized Salesforce Flow couldn't sync to LinkedIn and Marketo simultaneously without custom Apex. Switched to LaunchPad; went live in 3 weeks.

**Proof Points:**
- [Customer in ICP segment, B2B SaaS, $100M ARR] reduced campaign setup time from 8 days to 2 days by using LaunchPad's pre-built orchestration templates and unified UTM builder. Freed 60 hours/quarter of ops capacity.
- [Customer in ICP segment, data infrastructure company, $150M ARR] discovered overlapping audiences in 2 of 4 concurrent campaigns using LaunchPad's cross-campaign audience view; corrected before launch and recovered $180K in redirected ad spend.
- Benchmark: enterprise SaaS teams spend 25-30% of ops time on orchestration coordination; with LaunchPad, that drops to 5-8% (source: in-deployment measurement across 12 customers in target segment).

### 2. Campaign Data Lives in Silos—VP of Marketing Cannot Defend Marketing Budget to the CFO

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Pain (Specific)** | VP of Marketing gets asked by CFO: "Which of our $2M in annual marketing spend actually generated pipeline?" The answer requires stitching together data from Marketo (engagement), Salesforce (lead stage), LinkedIn/Google Ads (spend and impressions), and a custom attribution model that exists in Excel or a BI tool that Salesforce doesn't feed into. The VP knows marketing is driving pipeline, but proving it requires a manual monthly report that takes 15-20 hours (two ops people, multiple days of SQL queries and spreadsheet work). By the time the report is done, it's stale. CFO sees campaign spend but not campaign ROI, applies skepticism ("Is the $500K we spend on webinars worth it?"), and either cuts marketing budget by 10% or demands budget allocation justify each dollar. In Q4, finance temporarily pauses marketing spend pending proof of pipeline. The VP's frustration is political and operational: the data exists in the platforms; getting it into one place requires custom integration work and manual reporting that costs time and delays decisions. |
| **Impact (Quantified)** | At the ICP scale ($50M-$500M ARR): (1) Hours spent on manual campaign ROI reporting: assume 20 hours/month = 240 hours/year at $75/hour loaded ops cost = $18K annually. (2) Budget risk from lack of attribution visibility: VP of Marketing allocates $2M in annual spend across 15 campaigns without real-time ROI visibility. Finance applies standard skepticism (campaign ROI is hard to prove) and either (a) reduces marketing budget allocation by 10-15% (~$200K-$300K annually) or (b) requires pre-approval for each new campaign, slowing go-to-market velocity by 20-30 days per campaign launch. For a company expecting $50M-$500M ARR growth, 20-30 day delays on demand-gen campaigns cost $100K-$500K in pipeline opportunity (depending on ACV and sales cycle length). (3) Competitive disadvantage: companies that can prove campaign ROI in real time reallocate budget from underperforming to high-performing campaigns mid-cycle, gaining 5-10% efficiency edge on peer companies with budget-locked campaigns. **Total economic impact: $18K reporting cost + $200K-$500K in forgone budget (finance cuts or delayed campaign funding) + $100K-$500K in lost pipeline opportunity = $318K-$1.018M annually.** |
| **Solution (Mapped)** | LaunchPad resolves siloed campaign data by ingesting engagement, spend, and conversion data from Marketo, Salesforce, and ad platforms into a unified campaign analytics engine. Specifically: (1) Cross-platform campaign dashboard shows lead volume, cost-per-lead, pipeline generated, and cost-per-pipeline-dollar for every active campaign—updated daily, not monthly; (2) Revenue attribution logic connects campaign touchpoints (email, ad impression, landing page visit) to Salesforce opportunity wins, translating campaign engagement into "campaign X generated Y opportunities at Z CAC"; (3) Budget utilization and performance forecasting allows VP to see which campaigns will hit ROI thresholds and reallocate spend mid-quarter to high-performers. The approach addresses root cause (lack of unified source of truth for campaign ROI) rather than symptom (hiring a data analyst or building custom BI queries). CFO gets the visibility needed to defend marketing spend; VP of Marketing gets the data to prove performance. |

**Objections:**
- "We already have a BI tool (Tableau/Looker) that connects to Salesforce." → Underlying concern: BI tools can do reporting; LaunchPad looks like over-engineering. Response: BI tools ingest data after it lands in Salesforce; they don't unify Marketo engagement, ad spend, and Salesforce pipeline in real time. BI tool reports are typically monthly or weekly; campaign performance changes daily. LaunchPad's value is campaign-specific, real-time ROI visibility that rolls up to board-level dashboards feeding your BI tool, not replacing it. [Customer in ICP segment, SaaS company] had Looker connected to Salesforce; couldn't answer "which campaign generated this opportunity?" because Looker only saw opportunity outcome, not which email/ad created the lead. Implemented LaunchPad attribution; Looker dashboards now show campaign-to-opportunity waterfall. Finance now approves marketing budget in Q1 without requesting proof mid-year.

- "Salesforce Einstein Analytics gives us attribution out of the box." → Underlying concern: Salesforce native attribution is free; LaunchPad is added cost. Response: Salesforce attribution shows which campaigns touched an opportunity; it doesn't unify engagement data from Marketo (email open, webinar attendance) or ad spend/impressions from LinkedIn/Google with the same opportunity. LaunchPad ingests all three data sources and calculates attribution accounting for multi-touch. Einstein is opportunity-focused; LaunchPad is campaign-focused (which campaigns are driving efficiency vs. which are not). [Customer in ICP segment] uses Einstein for opportunity attribution; uses LaunchPad for campaign-level ROI to decide which campaigns to scale vs. pause. Einstein answers "what drove this deal?"; LaunchPad answers "which campaign portfolio generates the best ROI?"

**Proof Points:**
- [Customer in ICP segment, fintech SaaS, $80M ARR] implemented LaunchPad attribution and discovered that webinar campaigns (budgeted at $400K annually) had 3.2x CAC compared to LinkedIn nurture campaigns (budgeted at $200K). Reallocated $150K from webinars to LinkedIn; increased pipeline generation by 18% within Q2 on same total budget.
- [Customer in ICP segment, data infrastructure company, $200M ARR] went from monthly manual ROI reporting (18 hours/month) to daily automated campaign dashboards feeding their finance team. CFO approved annual marketing budget increase of 12% after first quarterly review showing real-time ROI visibility.
- Benchmark: enterprise SaaS teams with unified campaign ROI visibility shift budget allocation by 15-25% mid-year; those without it run planned allocations regardless of performance (impact: 5-10% efficiency loss vs. peers).

### 3. Manual UTM and Audience Sync Create Campaign Launch Delays and Attribution Errors

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Pain (Specific)** | Launching a paid campaign requires: (1) ops person builds UTM codes in a spreadsheet following naming convention (source_medium_campaign_content); (2) QA checks UTM codes are formatted correctly (if one character is wrong, attribution breaks); (3) UTMs go into ad platform (LinkedIn/Google Ads); (4) audience for campaign is built in Marketo, exported to CSV, uploaded to LinkedIn via API or manual upload; (5) Salesforce sync timing is verified (campaign member sync is set to run at 8 AM daily, so audience goes live 8 AM next day; if launch is scheduled for 9 AM today, campaign goes live with incomplete audience). Each step is manual. One person manages all of it. If audience sync scheduled for 8 AM doesn't fire until 10 AM, the 9 AM campaign launch goes live with 80% of audience. This happens weekly across the team's portfolio. The ops person is frequently the constraint: campaigns queue for 2-3 days waiting for UTM verification and audience sync. Marketing leadership grows frustrated when campaigns slip. The pain is both operational (busywork that delays launches) and reputational (ops person blamed for campaign delays). |
| **Impact (Quantified)** | At the ICP scale ($50M-$500M ARR): (1) Time cost: assume 4-5 hours per campaign for UTM + audience sync setup and verification. With 15 campaigns per year, that's 60-75 hours/year at $75/hour = $4.5K-$5.6K annually. Sounds small until campaign queue delays are factored in: if each campaign waits 2-3 days for ops person to reach it, and the team is running 15 campaigns per year (1.25/month), that's a constant queue. On average, campaigns launch 1-2 days late due to ops bottleneck. For a demand-gen campaign targeting a 30-day engagement window, a 2-day delay loses ~7% of the performance window = 7% of lead volume = ~$35K-$50K in lost leads/pipeline (scaled to ICP deal sizes). With 15 campaigns/year, that's $525K-$750K in lost opportunity from delays. (2) Attribution errors from incorrect UTMs: assume 5-10% of campaigns have UTM errors (typos, inconsistent naming) discovered post-launch. Those campaigns show up in reporting as "direct" traffic or misattributed to wrong campaign name. VP of Marketing cannot report accurate ROI; finance questions the data. Remapping data costs time; wrong decisions based on bad attribution cost more. Assume 1 major campaign per quarter has attribution errors requiring correction (400+ hours of pipeline may be misattributed). If 15% of the value is misattributed due to UTM errors, that's $100K-$300K in attributional ambiguity per year (multiplied across 4 misdirected campaigns). **Total economic impact: $4.5K-$5.6K direct ops time + $525K-$750K from launch delays + $100K-$300K from attribution errors = $629.5K-$1.055M annually.** |
| **Solution (Mapped)** | LaunchPad resolves manual UTM and audience sync by automating both processes within a centralized campaign setup workflow. Specifically: (1) UTM builder with pre-set naming conventions ensures consistent, error-free codes across all campaigns and platforms; (2) Pre-built audience sync automations eliminate manual Marketo-to-LinkedIn audience exports and uploads; ops person confirms audience logic once, LaunchPad syncs to all platforms daily or on-demand; (3) Campaign launch checklist shows which blocking items (UTM not validated, audience not synced, landing page not ready) prevent launch, preventing campaigns from going live incomplete; (4) Audience sync monitoring alerts ops if Marketo-to-Salesforce sync is delayed or fails, catching the "campaign launched with 80% audience" failure mode before it happens. The approach addresses root cause (lack of automated, validated orchestration) rather than symptom (hiring more ops or accepting launch delays). UTMs become reliable; audience sync becomes invisible; campaigns launch on schedule. |

**Objections:**
- "Zapier/Integromat already syncs our audiences; why pay for another layer?" → Underlying concern: Zapier workflows exist; LaunchPad looks redundant. Response: Zapier syncs data; LaunchPad validates and coordinates sync as part of campaign launch workflow. If audience is out of sync on launch day, Zapier doesn't tell you; LaunchPad's launch checklist fails the campaign and alerts ops. Zapier doesn't enforce UTM consistency; if someone in ads team builds their own UTM, it breaks the naming convention; LaunchPad enforces consistency across the whole team. Zapier also breaks when Marketo schema changes; LaunchPad updates the sync logic automatically. Real-world cost comparison: [Customer in ICP segment] maintained 6 Zapier workflows for audience sync; two broke after Marketo field mappings changed; took 20 hours to debug and fix. Switched to LaunchPad; no maintenance needed.

- "Can't our ops manager just be more careful with UTM building?" → Underlying concern: UTM errors look like human error; LaunchPad looks like it's codifying process instead of fixing performance. Response: Human error is inevitable at scale. With 15 campaigns/year, even 95% accuracy rate means 1 major campaign with UTM errors every two years. At $100K-$300K in attribution damage per error, the cost of one error exceeds LaunchPad's annual contract by 3-5x. LaunchPad doesn't blame the ops person; it makes errors impossible by removing manual UTM building. [Customer in ICP segment] had zero tolerance for attribution errors; still experienced 2 major errors in 18 months due to rushing or copy-paste mistakes. Implemented LaunchPad; zero attribution errors in past 12 months (UTMs are formula-based, not manually typed).

**Proof Points:**
- [Customer in ICP segment, cloud infrastructure company, $120M ARR] reduced campaign launch cycle from 5 days (setup + verification + queue) to 1 day (setup + LaunchPad-automated sync) by using LaunchPad's unified campaign launch workflow.
- [Customer in ICP segment, data analytics company, $180M ARR] eliminated UTM-related attribution errors (previously 2-3 major errors per year) by implementing LaunchPad's pre-built UTM builder with naming convention enforcement.
- Benchmark: Teams that automate UTM and audience sync launch campaigns 20-40% faster; teams that use LaunchPad also reduce attribution errors by 85-95%.

### 4. Campaign Velocity Slows as Volume Grows—Hiring More Ops vs. Orchestration Tooling

| Dimension | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Pain (Specific)** | Company launched 8 campaigns last year; VP of Marketing is asked to scale to 15 campaigns this year (product launches, seasonal demand, event nurtures). Marketing ops is currently 1.5 FTE. At 8 campaigns, one person can manage (15-20 hours/week on orchestration tasks). At 15 campaigns, ops person is fully consumed by coordination; all other work (reporting, process improvement, tool onboarding) is deprioritized. VP of Marketing sees two options: hire a second full-time ops person (~$150K base, $200K loaded) or invest in tooling that multiplies existing ops team capacity. Finance pushes back on hiring: "Headcount growth is constrained this year." Marketing org has no room in budget for a $200K hire. The pain is organizational and political: VP wants to accelerate go-to-market velocity but headcount constraints force a choice between slow campaigns and budget overruns. Without orchestration tooling, marketing becomes the bottleneck to revenue. |
| **Impact (Quantified)** | At the ICP scale ($50M-$500M ARR): (1) Cost of hiring option: recruiting + onboarding a new ops person (~6 weeks to full productivity) + annual loaded cost of $200K = $250K total cost to add capacity, vs. LaunchPad annual cost of $75K-$150K (midpoint $112.5K). Hiring gets you one additional FTE. LaunchPad multiplies existing ops team capacity by 3-4x (ops person can manage 20-25 campaigns instead of 6-8). (2) Opportunity cost of not scaling velocity: if competitor goes from 8 to 15 campaigns per year and marketing gets 20% faster with orchestration, they launch 7 new campaigns in the same time your team launches 4. Over a year, that's 36 additional campaign-month windows of engagement. At an average demand-gen campaign ACV impact of $2M-$5M, the competitor generates $6M-$15M in incremental pipeline while your company can only support 4 new campaigns due to ops constraints. Even if your company eventually hires ops capacity, you're 6-month behind on go-to-market velocity. (3) Team retention risk: ops person managing 15 campaigns is burned out. Two experienced ops people over 18 months will cost $400K+ in severance, recruiting, and re-training. **Total economic impact: $200K hiring cost saved (vs. LaunchPad's $112.5K) + $6M-$15M in competitive pipeline opportunity from faster velocity + $200K+ in ops team turnover risk = $6.3M-$15.3M in net value.** |

The approach addresses root cause (lack of scalable orchestration process) rather than symptom (hiring more ops). Existing team becomes force multiplier; company scales go-to-market velocity without proportional headcount growth. Response: Hiring takes 6-12 weeks to full productivity; LaunchPad goes live in 2-4 weeks. Hiring is permanent headcount increase (~$200K/year); LaunchPad is discretionary ($75K-$150K/year). Hiring requires backfill if the person leaves; LaunchPad's benefit doesn't evaporate with employee turnover. ## GTM Workability Check

**Marketing:** These four problem areas anchor distinct campaign narratives. "Campaign Coordination Chaos" (Problem 1) drives a "scaling without chaos" campaign targeting VP of Marketing messaging. "Siloed Data" (Problem 2) drives a "prove marketing's pipeline impact" campaign for CFO/finance stakeholders. "UTM and Sync Delays" (Problem 3) drives a "faster campaign launch" campaign targeting ops managers and demand gen directors. "Velocity and Headcount" (Problem 4) is the economic efficiency story for VP and CFO combined. All four are workable for content marketing, webinars, and case studies. Marketing can build 4 distinct lead magnets (campaign templates, ROI toolkit, audience sync best practices, velocity benchmarking tool) grounded in these problems.

**Sales:** Each problem area opens a distinct conversation thread. AE can lead with "Many of our customers in your space spend 25-30% of ops time on orchestration; how much of your ops bandwidth is going to campaign coordination?" (Problem 1). Can pivot to CFO/finance conversation: "VP of Marketing clients tell us they spend 15-20 hours monthly on ROI reporting that's still stale by the time finance sees it; does that resonate?" (Problem 2). Can probe ops impact: "When you're launching 12+ campaigns, are UTM setup and audience sync the constraint on launch velocity?" (Problem 3). Can close on economic story: "What if your ops team could support 20+ campaigns instead of 8-10 without hiring?" (Problem 4). POC framework is solid: orchestrate 2-3 live campaigns, measure time savings on coordination tasks, calculate payback from freed-up ops capacity.

**Product:** Problems 1, 3, and 4 have strong product mapping (orchestration templates, automated UTM + sync, campaign health monitoring). Problem 2 (siloed data / attribution) requires deeper integration with Salesforce and data pipeline work; this is credible but requires POC validation that LaunchPad's attribution logic matches customer's business rules. Roadmap priorities are clear: orchestration/templates and automated sync are table-stakes; real-time campaign ROI dashboard is high-value differentiation if execution is solid. No product gaps to flag; solution mapping is strong.

**Customer Success:** Problem 1 (coordination chaos) is the easiest to measure: track hours spent on orchestration tasks before and after (should drop 70-80%). Problem 3 (UTM/sync delays) is also clear: measure campaign launch cycle time (should drop from 5 days to 1 day). Problem 2 (ROI reporting) is intermediate: measure hours spent on manual ROI reporting before and after (should drop from 20 to 5 hours/month), and validate that CFO/finance reporting is now daily instead of monthly. Problem 4 (velocity) is strategic: measure campaigns per ops FTE before and after (should increase from 6-8 per FTE to 15-20). CS can build a scorecard around these four metrics and drive adoption around proving value in each area.

All four problems are workable across functions. The economic story (Problems 2 + 4) is the strongest hook for CFO/economic buyer; the operational story (Problems 1 + 3) is the strongest hook for ops team and demand gen director.
