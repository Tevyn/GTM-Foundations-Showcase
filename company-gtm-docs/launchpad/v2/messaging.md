# LaunchPad — Messaging Framework

## Positioning Spine
> For VP of Marketing and demand gen leaders at enterprise SaaS companies running 12+ campaigns across Marketo, Salesforce, and paid channels — LaunchPad is the campaign orchestration platform that coordinates the entire stack as one system, preserving existing tool investments while eliminating the coordination tax that scales with campaign volume. Unlike native platform orchestration, DIY automation, or stack consolidation, LaunchPad preserves the enterprise team's existing tool investments while eliminating the coordination tax that scales with campaign volume — because it was built for the above-stack layer those tools were never designed to occupy.

**How this anchors the messaging:** The narrative below leads with "coordination tax" — the invisible cost that grows with every campaign added. Every pillar traces back to the above-stack orchestration wedge. The voice speaks to marketing leaders who chose their tools for good reasons and are frustrated that those tools don't work together — we validate their past decisions while solving the coordination problem, never questioning their stack.

## The Story

### The Problem We're Solving
You're running 15 campaigns this quarter across Marketo, Salesforce, LinkedIn, and Google Ads. Every campaign requires manual audience syncing, UTM building, and cross-platform coordination — and one ops person can't keep pace. Last month a product launch went live with overlapping audiences and broken UTMs. The VP of Marketing can't tell the CFO which campaigns actually drove pipeline because the data lives in four different systems. Friday afternoons disappear into spreadsheets instead of strategy. And finance is asking whether the $2M marketing budget is actually working.

### Why Now
You just closed funding and the board expects marketing to scale pipeline contribution — not headcount. Your new VP of Marketing has a mandate to run 15+ campaigns this year, but the ops team that managed 8 campaigns last year is already at capacity. You've tried native Salesforce/Marketo orchestration (it doesn't span systems), Zapier workflows (they break when schemas change), and hiring (headcount is frozen). The campaign volume is growing regardless — the question is whether your coordination infrastructure keeps pace or becomes the bottleneck that limits go-to-market velocity.

### Our Promise
Your ops team manages 20+ campaigns with the same headcount. Campaigns launch in a day instead of five because the coordination — audience sync, UTM validation, launch readiness — happens automatically. Your VP of Marketing shows the CFO a daily dashboard connecting campaign spend to pipeline, and finance approves budget growth because they can finally see what's working. The tools you already invested in — Marketo, Salesforce, your ad platforms — work as one system instead of a collection of disconnected platforms you manually stitch together every week.

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. Above-Stack Coordination — "Your tools are powerful. The coordination between them is manual."
Marketo handles email. Salesforce handles pipeline. LinkedIn and Google handle demand. But nobody handles the coordination between them — the audience syncing, UTM consistency, launch sequencing, and cross-platform monitoring that every campaign requires. LaunchPad is the orchestration layer that makes your existing stack work as one system, eliminating the 25-30% of ops capacity currently consumed by manual coordination.

**Why this matters:** Cross-system coordination is the first and most decisive evaluation criterion for this buyer. They've already tried native platform tools (Salesforce Flow works within Salesforce but can't see Marketo or LinkedIn) and DIY automation (Zapier moves data but doesn't know whether a campaign is ready to launch). The coordination problem is the one they live with daily, and it's the problem no incumbent platform was designed to solve — because coordinating across competitors' tools isn't in any single vendor's interest.

**Proof point:** Customer ($150M ARR, data infrastructure) had campaigns queuing 2-3 days for ops coordination. Implemented LaunchPad in 3 weeks. Ops time on coordination dropped from 20 hours/week to 4. Campaign launch cycle compressed from 5 days to 1.5 days. Zero coordination failures in 6 months (vs. 2-3 major incidents per year previously).

### 2. Campaign Velocity Without Headcount — "Scale from 8 campaigns to 20 without hiring"
Your ops person is the bottleneck. At 8 campaigns, they manage. At 15, they're drowning. The traditional answer — hire another ops person — costs $200K loaded and takes 6 weeks to full productivity. LaunchPad costs half that, goes live in 2-4 weeks, and multiplies your existing team's capacity by 3-4x. One ops person managing LaunchPad handles more campaigns than two ops people managing spreadsheets.

**Why this matters:** This is the economic story the CFO evaluates. At $75K-$150K annually vs. $200K for a headcount hire, the TCO argument is clear. But the real value isn't cost savings — it's velocity. Companies using orchestration tooling manage 2.5-3.5x more campaigns per ops FTE than those using manual processes. That velocity advantage compounds: 7 more campaigns per year means 7 more demand gen windows, 7 more product launches, 7 more opportunities to capture pipeline ahead of competitors.

**Proof point:** Customer ($100M ARR, B2B SaaS) scaled from 8 to 18 concurrent campaigns per year using existing 1.5 FTE ops team + LaunchPad. Would have required hiring a third person ($200K) to manage that volume manually. Net savings: $87.5K annually plus 25% faster campaign velocity.

### 3. Campaign ROI Visibility — "Prove to finance which campaigns drive pipeline"
Your VP of Marketing knows campaigns are driving pipeline but can't prove it — because engagement lives in Marketo, spend in ad platforms, and conversions in Salesforce. Assembling the picture takes 20 hours a month of manual reporting that's stale by the time finance sees it. LaunchPad connects campaign spend to pipeline outcomes across all platforms, updating daily — so when the CFO asks "is the $2M budget working?" the answer is a dashboard, not a spreadsheet.

**Why this matters:** This pillar opens the CFO door. Budget protection and growth depend on ROI visibility — VPs of Marketing who can prove campaign efficiency in real time reallocate budget mid-cycle to high performers (5-10% efficiency gain over peers) and avoid the 10-15% budget cuts that finance applies when marketing can't demonstrate impact. It's also the pillar that converts POC success into expansion: once the team sees which campaigns generate pipeline, they want more orchestration and attribution across more campaigns.

**Proof point:** Customer ($80M ARR, fintech SaaS) discovered webinar campaigns had 3.2x CAC vs. LinkedIn nurture. Reallocated $150K; increased pipeline 18% same quarter on same budget. CFO approved 12% marketing budget increase after first quarterly review with daily LaunchPad dashboards.

## Voice & Tone

### Voice Characteristics
| Characteristic | Sounds Like | Doesn't Sound Like |
|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Operationally candid** | "Your ops person is spending Fridays in spreadsheets instead of optimizing campaigns" | "Unlock the power of unified marketing" |
| **Specific about scale** | "At 12+ campaigns, manual coordination breaks — audience overlap, UTM errors, launch delays" | "As your marketing grows, you need better tools" |
| **Economically grounded** | "LaunchPad costs half what an ops hire costs and goes live in 2 weeks, not 6" | "Invest in the future of marketing operations" |
| **Stack-respectful** | "Your Marketo and Salesforce are powerful — the coordination between them shouldn't be manual" | "Replace your fragmented marketing tools" |

### Tone by Context
- **Website / marketing content:** Lead with the coordination problem, not the product. "Your martech stack doesn't have a coordination problem — it has a coordination gap." Use operational specifics (campaign counts, hours lost, launch delays) that make the reader nod in recognition.
- **Sales conversations:** Start with their campaign volume and stack complexity. "Walk me through what happens when you launch a campaign that touches Marketo, LinkedIn, and Salesforce — who coordinates that?" Let them describe the pain. Confirm with benchmarks: "Most teams at your scale spend 25-30% of ops time on coordination."
- **Customer success:** Frame around operational metrics: coordination hours recovered, campaign launch velocity, attribution accuracy. "You went from 5-day launches to 1-day — here's what that means for next quarter's campaign plan."

### Buyer Language Patterns
| Buyers Say | We've Been Saying | Use Their Language |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| "Campaign chaos" | "Fragmented campaign management" | "The coordination chaos that comes with running 15+ campaigns" |
| "I'm the bottleneck" | "Ops team capacity constraints" | "Your ops person shouldn't be the bottleneck" |
| "Can't prove ROI to finance" | "Unified attribution analytics" | "Show finance which campaigns drive pipeline — daily, not monthly" |
| "Spreadsheets for something that should be automated" | "Manual campaign coordination workflows" | "Stop managing campaigns in spreadsheets" |
| "We need orchestration, not another tool" | "Campaign orchestration platform" | "The orchestration layer your stack is missing" |

**The vendor test:** If you replaced "LaunchPad" with "HubSpot" or "Marketo" and the messaging still made sense, it's too generic. Every message should depend on the "above-stack coordination" wedge. If it could apply to a single-system platform, redo it.

## Talk Track Guidance

### "What do you do?"
"You know how enterprise marketing teams end up with Marketo, Salesforce, LinkedIn Ads, Google Ads — great tools individually, but nobody coordinates between them? So your ops person spends a quarter of their time manually syncing audiences, building UTMs, and making sure campaigns don't overlap. LaunchPad is the orchestration layer that sits above all of it and handles that coordination automatically. Customers go from managing 8 campaigns at capacity to running 20+ with the same team. One of our fintech customers reallocated $150K in ad spend after seeing which campaigns actually drove pipeline — first time they could see that in real time."

### "How are you different?"
Lead with the buyer's situation, not the feature comparison: "The real question is where the coordination problem lives. Salesforce Flow and Marketo Journey Orchestrator are great within their own systems — but they can't see audience state across Salesforce and LinkedIn and Marketo simultaneously. Zapier connects tools but doesn't understand campaigns — it won't tell you if a campaign is about to launch with an incomplete audience. And HubSpot consolidation means ripping out your Salesforce and Marketo investment. We're the layer above all of them — purpose-built for the cross-system coordination that none of those tools were designed to handle."

### Language to Avoid
- **"Marketing automation platform"** — puts you in the Marketo/HubSpot frame and you'll lose. Say "campaign orchestration layer" to activate the above-stack evaluation.
- **"Our platform integrates with..."** — sounds like every integration tool. Say "sits above your existing stack and coordinates between them" to emphasize the layer position.
- **"We help marketing teams be more efficient"** — generic, fails the vendor test. Say "we eliminate the coordination tax that grows with every campaign you add."
- **"Centralized marketing hub"** — sounds like HubSpot's positioning. Say "orchestration layer" to differentiate from consolidation plays.
- **"AI-powered campaign optimization"** — not the wedge. The wedge is above-stack coordination. Lead there; optimization is a feature, not the message.
