# LaunchPad — Ideal Customer Profile: Enterprise SaaS Tech Segment

## Overview
This ICP targets high-growth SaaS companies in enterprise technology (Series C+, $50M-$500M ARR) running 15+ concurrent campaigns annually across email, digital ads, webinars, and events—a scale that breaks single-tool approaches. They operate fragmented stacks (Marketo/HubSpot Enterprise + Salesforce + Google Ads/LinkedIn) with marketing teams of 8+, and have a VP of Marketing accountable for pipeline contribution. Core fit: LaunchPad enables campaign velocity without hiring more ops headcount, and unified analytics provide precision required to justify marketing spend growth to finance.

## Primary Segment

### Firmographics
**Industry:** Enterprise B2B SaaS (software, cloud infrastructure, data/analytics, fintech)
**Company Size:** 200-2,000 employees
**ARR/Revenue:** $50M-$500M ARR
**Geography:** North America primary (US/Canada), secondary EMEA
**Growth Stage:** Series C or later; sustainable profitability or well-funded

### Technographics
**Marketing stack:** 6+ integrated tools including Marketo Enterprise or HubSpot Enterprise, Salesforce CRM, LinkedIn/Google Ads, proprietary or customized landing page builder, and CDP/audience sync platform.

**Why this signals fit:** Marketo/Salesforce/LinkedIn architecture indicates volume complexity and orchestration pain. Campaign data lives across 4+ systems with no unified source of truth; each new campaign requires manual setup, UTM verification, and audience sync coordination. LaunchPad's value is highest because it sits above the stack as a unified orchestration layer.

**Non-fit signals:** Single-tool stacks (Marketo-only, HubSpot-only) or DIY Zapier solutions indicate either lower campaign volume or acceptance of manual coordination—not a fit.

### Buying Triggers
- **Recent funding round or Series C completion** — Budget release, revenue acceleration mandate, pressure to scale without doubling marketing headcount
- **New product launch or market entry** — 3-5 concurrent campaigns required; manual orchestration becomes bottleneck; need for precise audience segmentation
- **VP of Marketing hired in past 12 months** — New leader audits MarTech stack, identifies orchestration gaps as efficiency win, has mandate to improve pipeline contribution
- **Campaign volume expansion to 12+ active annual campaigns** — Below 12/year, manual coordination is manageable; above 12/year, coordination windows are missed, audiences overlap, budgets aren't optimized
- **Failed campaign SLAs or revenue attribution gaps** — Missed quarter-end campaign due to coordination delays; inability to prove ROI on paid spend

Ideal timing: Experienced orchestration pain in last quarter, allocated Q2/Q3 budget for tooling, new leadership with political will to fix it.

### Buying Process Notes
**Deal cycle:** 6-9 months
**POC expectation:** 8 in 10 deals require 30-45 day POC orchestrating 2-3 active campaigns
**Committee:** VP of Marketing (champion, ROI focus), Director of Demand Gen (execution owner), Marketing Operations Manager (integration/security requirements), CRO/CFO observer (pipeline attribution)
**Security review:** 4-6 weeks; requires SOC 2 Type II, data residency options, IP whitelisting

## Pain Points (Specific to This Segment)

- **Campaign coordination breaks under volume:** Concurrent campaigns (product launch + seasonal demand gen + event nurture) create audience overlap, duplicate touches, inconsistent tracking. Result: wasted ad spend, attribution confusion, inability to optimize budget in real time. Economic buyer loses visibility into whether marketing spend is generating incremental pipeline.

- **Campaign data lives in silos, preventing ROI proof:** Marketo shows engagement, Salesforce shows lead stage, LinkedIn shows impressions, Google Ads shows costs—no single system aggregates to "campaign X generated Y leads at Z CAC." VP of Marketing cannot defend budget allocation to the board; CFO questions marketing spend efficiency.

- **UTM and audience sync are manual, creating speed gaps:** Each campaign requires manual UTM building, schema verification, and audience list sync to Ad Manager/LinkedIn or Salesforce updates. One person cannot keep pace with 15+ campaigns. Some go live with wrong UTMs; audience sync delays lose ad targeting windows. Marketing loses days per quarter to this busywork.

- **Campaign velocity slows as volume grows:** Launching a campaign requires coordination across teams: "Is the audience in Marketo?" "Landing page ready?" "UTM set up?" "Salesforce sync ready?" Without orchestration, ops person becomes bottleneck. Scaling velocity requires hiring more ops; orchestration enables it without headcount.

- **Budget optimization is reactive, not real-time:** Marketing cannot shift budget from underperforming to high-performing campaigns mid-cycle because data isn't available until manual reporting. Campaigns run planned budgets regardless of performance signals. CFO sees wasted spend; CMO sees inability to be agile.

## Qualification Framework (Sales)

**Q1: Campaign volume and complexity**
*Question:* "Walk me through how many concurrent campaigns you're running in an average quarter. What does a typical campaign touch look like—email, ads, landing page, events?"
*Listen for:* 10+ concurrent campaigns; multiple channels per campaign; mentions of coordinating across Marketo/Salesforce/ad team; "we have to manage this manually"
*What it tells you:* Whether orchestration pain is real or still emerging. 20+ campaigns + no unified coordination = active efficiency loss.

**Q2: Technology stack and integration friction**
*Question:* "What's your current marketing tech stack? Walk me through how a campaign moves from Marketo or HubSpot to Salesforce to Ad Manager."
*Listen for:* Marketo Enterprise, Salesforce, LinkedIn/Google Ads, manual handoffs, "a spreadsheet tracks this," "Salesforce sync timing is a headache," "our ops person manages the integration"
*What it tells you:* Whether they have the integration complexity LaunchPad solves. Marketo/Salesforce/LinkedIn = acute pain. HubSpot + embedded tools may not have it.

**Q3: Trigger and urgency**
*Question:* "What changed recently that's making this a priority now? New product, new funding, new marketing leader, or campaign deadline you nearly missed?"
*Listen for:* Recency signals: "new VP of Marketing," "just closed funding," "three campaigns ran concurrently and coordination broke," "CFO asked us to prove ROI on paid spend"
*What it tells you:* Deal timing and real urgency. Recent trigger events (hire, funding, failed execution) = willingness to commit.

**Q4: Pain-to-business-impact connection**
*Question:* "When campaigns run in separate systems without coordination, what's the effect? How does that show up in pipeline, team capacity, or conversations with finance?"
*Listen for:* Operational pain connecting to business outcomes: "we miss campaign windows," "finance questions if paid spend works," "our ops person is a bottleneck," "thinking about hiring when we should scale through tooling"
*What it tells you:* Whether pain reaches economic buyer concerns (pipeline, efficiency, ROI visibility) or stays at team-level frustration. This filters advancement readiness.

## Negative ICP (Not a Fit)

| Profile | Why Not | Disqualification Signal |
|---------|---------|------------------------|
| HubSpot-only or Marketo-only users, <50 employees | No integration complexity; low ACV potential | "Run everything through HubSpot; no orchestration pain" or "team is 3 people" |
| Large financial services with 40+ compliance requirements | Long security reviews, high customization risk, non-standard deal | Mentions data residency, complex RFP requirements, 6+ month approval cycles |
| Series A-B with <$10M ARR | Low budget, small campaigns, future fit not current | ARR is single-digit millions or marketing team <3 people |
| DIY Zapier orchestration already in place | Direct competition; low willingness to pay for managed solution | "Built our own Zapier/Integromat workflows" or "don't want to add another tool" |
| Agency-outsourced marketing operations | Agency owns execution; no internal decision power; high complexity | "Our agency handles all campaigns" or "they manage our Marketo setup" |

**Near-miss handling:** Good tech stack fit but <$30M ARR = future fit; explore growth trajectory. CMO with mandate but single-tool stack = not immediate but may expand if positioning connects to growth plans.
