# PeopleGraph — Messaging Framework

## Positioning Spine
> For CHROs and CFOs at high-growth tech companies facing the workforce planning chaos of doubling headcount, PeopleGraph is the predictive workforce intelligence platform that shows who's at risk, where plans are drifting, and where pay equity is eroding — before these become crises. Unlike Workday analytics (retrospective) or Visier (built for steady-state enterprise), PeopleGraph is purpose-built for the dynamics of rapid growth, where attrition patterns shift quarterly, planning assumptions break mid-year, and compensation benchmarks move faster than annual reviews can track.

**How this anchors the messaging:** Every message below traces back to one idea: rapid growth breaks workforce planning, and PeopleGraph provides the predictive visibility to manage it. The narrative arc starts with the buyer's experience of growth-stage chaos, not with the product. The pillars map to the three crises the positioning identifies (attrition, planning, pay equity). The voice speaks to leaders who are scaling fast and anxious about losing their best people — confident and specific, never generic or aspirational.

## The Story

### The Problem We're Solving
You're doubling headcount in 18 months. Some teams retain nearly everyone; others are bleeding talent at 20%+ and you don't know why until someone gives notice. Your headcount budget is built on last year's assumptions, but attrition is running 3 points higher than plan and hiring ramp is slower than modeled. By April, you're choosing between a hiring freeze and burning cash. Meanwhile, the engineers you hired last year just found out the person who started six months after them negotiated $12K more — and now they're updating their LinkedIn profiles.

### Why Now
Series B funding created a mandate to scale efficiently, not just fast. The board wants to see that growth capital is being deployed into a workforce plan that holds, not into reactive backfill hiring. Every quarter you operate without predictive visibility, the gap between your plan and reality widens — and the cost compounds. Companies in your cohort that deployed predictive workforce intelligence during their scaling phase held attrition flat while peers saw 2-3 point spikes. The window to get ahead of this is the next two quarters, before the next funding milestone forces a look at headcount efficiency.

### Our Promise
You see who's at risk of leaving 60 days before they give notice — enough time to have a real conversation with their manager. Your CFO and CHRO run headcount scenarios together instead of arguing over spreadsheet assumptions. When a pay gap exists, you find it in a dashboard, not in an exit interview. Growth still feels fast, but it stops feeling chaotic — because you're making decisions from data, not from surprises.

## Messaging Pillars

### 1. Predictive Attrition Visibility — "See who's at risk before they give notice"
Your engagement survey tells you how people felt last month. PeopleGraph tells you who's likely to leave in the next 90 days — combining compensation positioning, tenure patterns, manager effectiveness signals, and peer cohort data to surface at-risk employees while there's still time to act. This shifts retention from reactive backfill to proactive coaching.

**Why this matters:** Unplanned attrition is the #1 concern for CHROs and operating partners at rapid-growth companies. At $200K replacement cost per person and 15-18% annual attrition rates in this segment, preventing even 2-3 key departures pays for the platform. This is also PeopleGraph's strongest differentiator — Workday shows who left last quarter; engagement surveys lag reality by weeks; PeopleGraph predicts who's about to leave using behavioral and market signals those tools can't access.

**Proof point:** Customer with 2,800 employees predicted 65% of at-risk cohort 60+ days in advance. Of those flagged, 40% had meaningful retention conversations (role adjustment, comp adjustment, development plan). Year-one result: unplanned attrition dropped 3.5 percentage points, preventing $140K+ in replacement costs.

### 2. Dynamic Headcount Planning — "Stop guessing your way through growth"
Your headcount budget assumes 80% retention and a 2-month hiring ramp. But what happens when engineering attrition hits 18% instead of 10%, or sales takes 4 months to ramp? PeopleGraph lets your CFO and CHRO model these scenarios together — accounting for attrition by department, hiring velocity, and compensation changes — so you reforecast mid-year instead of discovering a problem in Q3.

**Why this matters:** The CFO at a Series B company has the budget authority and the most acute version of this pain: inaccurate forecasting means either under-hiring (missed growth targets, product delays) or over-hiring (cash burn, margin compression). A model that improves forecast accuracy from ±15% to ±5% is worth $500K-$1.5M in prevented waste. This pillar opens the CFO door in deals where the CHRO is the champion but budget approval sits in finance.

**Proof point:** Customer (2,200 employees, $80M ARR, 35% growth target) discovered mid-year they were tracking 3.5 points higher attrition than plan. Using PeopleGraph scenario modeling, they re-forecasted and adjusted Q3-Q4 hiring strategy without a freeze. Met headcount targets within 50 people of plan, vs. historical 150-person band of error. Estimated savings: $2.5M in prevented over-hiring.

### 3. Real-Time Pay Equity — "Find pay gaps before they find you"
When you hire 300 people in a year, pay consistency breaks. External hires negotiate 8-12% above the benchmark; internal peers discover the gap during exit interviews or on Blind. PeopleGraph continuously monitors every employee's compensation against market rates and internal peers, surfacing gaps as they form — not months after they've driven your best people to update their resumes.

**Why this matters:** Pay equity has a compounding cost — one underpaid senior IC who leaves creates a $200K replacement cost, but the real damage is when knowledge of pay gaps spreads through exit interviews and Glassdoor. Companies in this segment with 5-8% of their workforce in discoverable pay gaps see incremental attrition that costs $500K-$1.5M annually. This pillar connects to both the retention story (keeps people) and the compliance story (board-level DEI accountability).

**Proof point:** Customer (3,100 employees, $180M ARR) ran pay equity audit and discovered 9% of workforce below 40th percentile of market. Targeted adjustment for top 3 salary bands cost $1.2M. Result: attrition in adjusted cohort dropped from 22% to 14%, preventing ~40 departures = $8M in replacement costs. ROI: 6.7x in year one.

## Voice & Tone

### Voice Characteristics
| Characteristic | Sounds Like | Doesn't Sound Like |
|---------------|-------------|-------------------|
| **Specific** | "Companies at your stage see 15-18% unplanned attrition in year 2 of rapid growth" | "Many companies struggle with employee retention" |
| **Forward-looking** | "See who's at risk 60 days before they give notice" | "Comprehensive workforce analytics and reporting" |
| **Empathetic to growth anxiety** | "Growth should feel fast, not chaotic" | "Optimize your human capital management" |
| **Evidence-grounded** | "Customers hold attrition flat during the scaling phase when peers see 2-3 point spikes" | "We're the leading people analytics platform" |

### Tone by Context
- **Website / marketing content:** Lead with the buyer's situation ("You're doubling headcount...") not the product ("PeopleGraph is..."). Use quantified impact in headlines. Write for the CHRO who's been caught off-guard by a departure this month.
- **Sales conversations:** Lead with questions about their growth plan and attrition experience. Use the pain → consequence chain conversationally: "When someone leaves unexpectedly at your stage, what does the backfill process look like?" Let the buyer describe the pain; confirm it with data.
- **Customer success:** Frame QBRs around outcomes: "Here's where attrition risk shifted this quarter. Here's the gap between your headcount plan and reality. Here's where pay positioning has changed." Never frame around feature usage.

### Buyer Language Patterns
| Buyers Say | We've Been Saying | Use Their Language |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------------|
| "We don't see people leaving until they give notice" | "Predictive attrition analytics" | "See who's at risk before they leave" |
| "Our headcount forecast is a guess" | "Dynamic workforce planning" | "Stop guessing — model it" |
| "We found out about the pay gap in an exit interview" | "Compensation benchmarking and pay equity" | "Find pay gaps before they find you" |
| "Culture feels chaotic during growth" | "Workforce intelligence for scaling organizations" | "Growth that feels fast, not chaotic" |
| "I need math, not assumptions" | "Data-driven workforce decisions" | "Replace assumptions with scenarios" |

**The vendor test:** If you replaced "PeopleGraph" with "Visier" or "Workday Analytics" and the messaging still made sense, it's too generic. Every message should depend on the "predictive + growth-stage dynamics" wedge. If it could apply to steady-state enterprise analytics, redo it.

## Talk Track Guidance

### "What do you do?"
"You know how when companies double headcount, they start losing people they didn't expect to lose — and by the time they find out, it's too late to do anything about it? PeopleGraph gives CHROs and CFOs at scaling companies the ability to see who's at risk of leaving, where the headcount plan is drifting, and where pay gaps are forming — before any of that turns into a crisis. One customer predicted 65% of their at-risk employees two months ahead and dropped attrition 3.5 points in year one."

### "How are you different?"
Lead with the buyer's criterion, not the feature: "The question is really whether you need backward-looking analytics or forward-looking intelligence. Workday is great at telling you what already happened — who left, what you spent, where headcount stands today. We tell you what's about to happen — who's likely to leave in the next 90 days, what your headcount will actually look like if attrition shifts, where pay gaps are forming right now. For companies in steady state, Workday analytics might be enough. For companies doubling headcount, the difference between knowing what happened and knowing what's coming is the difference between reactive backfill and proactive retention."

### Language to Avoid
- **"People analytics platform"** — puts you in the Visier/Workday frame. Say "predictive workforce intelligence" to activate the forward-looking evaluation criterion.
- **"Our solution leverages AI/ML to..."** — vendor-speak that every competitor uses. Say what it actually does: "predicts who's at risk of leaving 60 days out."
- **"Comprehensive workforce management"** — signals steady-state enterprise, not growth-stage urgency. Say "built for the dynamics of rapid growth."
- **"We help companies make better decisions about their people"** — the vendor test fails immediately. Be specific: "We help CHROs see attrition risk before it shows up in a resignation email."
- **"Data-driven HR"** — too broad, too 2018. Say "predictive visibility" to anchor in the value wedge.
