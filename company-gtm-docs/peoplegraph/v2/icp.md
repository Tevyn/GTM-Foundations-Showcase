# PeopleGraph — Ideal Customer Profile (V2)

## Overview

High-growth tech companies in Series B–C stage where headcount is doubling in 18–24 months and existing HR infrastructure can't keep pace. These organizations have sophisticated HR stacks (Workday or equivalent) but lack predictive capabilities and planning rigor to manage growth without attrition spikes. Core thesis: rapid growth without planning = unplanned turnover + compensation inequity + manager burnout. PeopleGraph addresses all three.

## Primary Segment

### Firmographics

**Industry:** Technology (software, SaaS, fintech, healthtech, edtech)

**Company Size:** 1,000–5,000 employees (post-Series B, pre-IPO scaling phase)

**Revenue:** $50M–$500M ARR

**Geography:** US-primary with secondary presence in London/Singapore (tech hubs where hiring competition is fiercest)

**Key signals:**
- Series B+ funding within past 18 months
- Headcount growth targets 30%+ YoY
- Talent-competitive markets (SF, NYC, London, Seattle)

### Technographics

**Tech stack requirements:**
- **HRIS:** Workday Enterprise or equivalent (BambooHR or ADP is insufficient — these companies have grown out of mid-market tooling)
- **Recruiting:** Greenhouse, Lever, or Workday Recruiting integration (indicates recruiting velocity and structured hiring)
- **Compensation management:** Radford, PayScale, or basic salary bands in Workday (signals pay benchmarking maturity; not ad-hoc)
- **Business intelligence:** Tableau, Looker, or Sisense already in use (indicates data literacy and infrastructure for custom analytics)

**Why this stack signals fit:**
- Workday + recruiting + BI = sophisticated ops layer lacking analytics/modeling that PeopleGraph provides
- Absence signals either manual-process-heavy company or insufficient growth urgency

### Buying Triggers

1. **Recent funding event (Series B+)** — Funding round closed in past 18 months, creating mandate to scale operations and capital to invest in infrastructure
   - Why it matters: Brings CFO + CHRO accountability for scaling efficiency
   - Threshold: Without growth capital, urgency is lower; small seed-stage companies won't see value

2. **Headcount growth target exceeds 30% YoY** — Public or internal 3-year plan that doubles headcount by 2027–2028
   - Why it matters: Manual headcount planning becomes impossible; unplanned attrition cascades into hiring delays
   - Threshold: Below 15% growth, hiring can be managed manually; below 20%, management time spent on planning is acceptable

3. **Documented attrition spike or manager turnover** — Unplanned departures of ICs or engineering managers in past 12 months (signals predictive value)
   - Why it matters: Leadership wants early warning system; sets up buying case around retention value
   - Threshold: Below 15% annual turnover, perceived as "normal" rather than crisis; visibility doesn't drive budget

4. **Compensation benchmarking gap identified** — CHRO or CFO flagged pay equity analysis as part of recent board discussion, ESG initiative, or audit
   - Why it matters: Creates accountability to address gaps; ties to retention risk (high performers leave if underpaid)
   - Threshold: Without board/CFO pressure, compensation work stays on backlog

5. **Manager effectiveness questions** — Engagement survey or skip-level feedback revealed manager quality variance (suggests some teams retain talent, others bleed it)
   - Why it matters: Coaching or replacement decision needs data; ties to attrition root cause
   - Threshold: Anecdotal observations are insufficient; formal data collection indicates readiness to act

### Buyer Roles

| Role | Title(s) | What They Care About | Their Role in the Deal |
|------|----------|---------------------|----------------------|
| **Champion / Execution Owner** | VP of People, CHRO, Director of People Operations | Time to implement, reducing manual reporting work, ability to run quick headcount scenarios, making managers' lives easier through better data | Drives internal consensus around tooling; often first contact; cares about operational value and team bandwidth |
| **Economic Buyer** | CFO, VP of Finance | Headcount planning accuracy (avoiding budget overruns), attrition cost impact, ROI and payback on analytics investment | Approves budget; needs to see link between better planning and cost control or revenue stability |
| **Execution Ally** | VP of Engineering, VP of Sales (any head of department scaling fast) | Predictive visibility into their own team's attrition risk, manager effectiveness data for 1:1 feedback | Validates pain, advocates for solution based on their own headcount challenges |

### Deal Economics

**ACV Range:** $120K–$300K (enterprise deployment with multiple departments, full Workday integration, custom dashboards)

**Deal Cycle:** 5–7 months (typical for enterprise with security review and board approval)

**Buying Process Notes:**
- POC standard in 7/10 deals (security review + data integration validation, 4–6 weeks)
- Board approval required in >60% of Series B+ companies (adds 2–3 weeks)
- Budget allocated at board level or through annual planning (not a "discretionary IT purchase")
- Multiple stakeholders (People + Finance + one operating partner) must align

## Pain Points (Specific to This Segment)

Each pain is traced from mechanism → why it's acute for this segment → business consequence:

- **Unplanned attrition during scaling:** As headcount doubles, some teams retain talent while others see 20%+ turnover. Without early warning of at-risk employees, replacement hiring can't keep pace, leading to understaffing → delayed product roadmap or missed sales targets → direct revenue impact.

- **Headcount planning inaccuracy:** Finance and CHRO build headcount budgets by gut and historical hiring rate, not by modeling attrition, department mix, or productivity shifts. Bad forecasts lead to either hiring freezes (missed growth targets) or over-hiring (cash burn escalation and margin compression) → board pressure and capital inefficiency.

- **Compensation equity blind spot:** Rapid hiring introduces pay inconsistency (external hire negotiates higher rate than internal peer in same role). Without benchmarking, leadership discovers disparities during exit interviews or engagement surveys. Unaddressed inequity drives retention of lower-paid employees → revenue impact and diversity goals stalled.

- **Manager quality variance affecting retention:** Some managers retain entire teams; others see 30% turnover. Without data linking manager effectiveness to retention, leadership can't coach or replace underperforming managers → talented ICs leave, and replacement costs spike.

- **Data fragmentation across HRIS, recruiting, and BI:** People data lives in Workday, recruiting pipeline in Greenhouse, compensation analysis in spreadsheets or PayScale, and custom reports in Tableau. Assembling a single headcount forecast requires manual joins across 3+ tools → CHRO and finance ops spend 15+ hours/month on ad-hoc reporting instead of strategy.

## Qualification Framework (Sales)

Ask these 4 questions in a discovery call to assess fit. Reps should carry this in their head; each question should take 2–3 minutes.

1. **Situational opener — Growth context:**
   - *Question:* "Tell me about your headcount plan for the next 18 months. How many net new people are you bringing on?"
   - *Listen for:* Specific growth targets (30%+), funding event that triggered planning, revenue/product milestone driving hiring
   - *What it tells you:* If growth is <20% or aspirational (not funded), pain isn't acute. If growth is real and accelerating, they're in the right window.

2. **Pain depth — Attrition visibility:**
   - *Question:* "When someone leaves your team unexpectedly, how do you find out? Walk me through that conversation."
   - *Listen for:* Surprise/reactivity ("we don't see it coming"), scope of impact ("affects the team's sprint"), length of replacement time ("takes 3 months to backfill")
   - *What it tells you:* If they catch it early or don't see unplanned departures, pain isn't real. If it's a surprise that cascades, this is a core pain point.

3. **Technographic confirmation — Infrastructure maturity:**
   - *Question:* "You mentioned Workday — what else sits alongside it? Do you have recruiting tools, BI platform, or compensation benchmarking software?"
   - *Listen for:* Workday + Greenhouse/Lever + Tableau/Looker (high fit), Workday alone (lower pain), legacy HRIS or spreadsheet (wrong buyer)
   - *What it tells you:* Sophistication of their stack signals whether they have data literacy and infrastructure to use predictive analytics.

4. **Economic trigger — Decision authority:**
   - *Question:* "Who decided that scaling operations was a priority? Was that your leadership, the board, or a finance conversation?"
   - *Listen for:* Recent board meeting, CFO mandate, CHRO brought in after funding (recency and authority signal urgency)
   - *What it tells you:* If the trigger is recent and came from above the CHRO, deal velocity will be faster. If it's bottom-up and tactical, expect longer sales cycle.

## Negative ICP (Not a Fit)

| Profile | Why Not | Disqualification Signal |
|---------|---------|------------------------|
| **Pre-seed or early-stage tech (<500 employees, <$10M ARR)** | Low ACV, no budget allocated to analytics infrastructure, people ops not yet formalized | "We're still figuring out our team structure" or "HR is managed part-time by ops" |
| **Mature manufacturing or retail (stable headcount, low attrition)** | Slow growth = low planning urgency; low manager burnout relative to target segment; existing workforce still concentrated in legacy tools | "We hire slowly and people stay a long time" or "We haven't replaced our legacy payroll system" |
| **Organizations without HRIS/Workday** | Poor data foundation; manual HR processes mean data quality too low for predictive analytics; wrong buyer (ops manager, not CHRO) | "We manage people in spreadsheets" or "We're not ready to invest in HR tech" |
| **Non-growth industries (professional services, government, non-profit)** | Different headcount dynamics; budgeting cycles are long and conservative; people analytics is not a strategic priority | No mention of growth targets; "People planning is stable year-to-year" |
| **Organizations still in POC/evaluation with Workday** | Not operationally ready; energy is on implementation, not analytics | "We're 6 months into Workday implementation" or "We haven't migrated all our data yet" |

**How to handle near-misses:**
Bootstrapped growth without recent funding: lower-urgency but viable. Lead with operational efficiency (less reporting work) vs. growth planning. Legacy HRIS with high growth: acknowledge data foundation gap and suggest revisiting after HRIS upgrade.
