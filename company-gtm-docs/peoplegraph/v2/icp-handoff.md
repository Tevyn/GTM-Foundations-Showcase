# ICP Handoff: Context for Downstream Pillars — PeopleGraph V2

## For Problem-Solution Fit

**Pain areas with business-consequence chains:**

1. **Unplanned attrition during scaling** → Employee loss → Replacement hiring delays → Understaffed teams → Delayed product roadmap or missed sales targets → Direct revenue impact

2. **Headcount planning inaccuracy** → Budget mismatch (over/under-hiring) → Cash burn escalation (over) or growth targets missed (under) → Board pressure and margin compression → Capital inefficiency

3. **Compensation equity blind spot** → Internally inconsistent pay discovered during exits → Retention of lower-paid talent → Attrition of better performers → Revenue impact and stalled diversity goals

4. **Manager quality variance** → High-performing managers retain teams; underperformers see 30% turnover → Leadership can't coach/replace without data → Talented ICs leave, replacement costs spike → Revenue disruption

5. **Data fragmentation across HR systems** → Manual reporting consuming 15+ hours/month → CHRO/ops spending time on data assembly vs. strategy → Slower decision cycles → Delayed scaling strategy

**Buyer outcomes by role:**
- CHRO/VP People: Reduce manual reporting, model scenarios quickly, lower attrition, identify pay gaps
- CFO: Headcount planning accuracy, attrition cost visibility, ROI on infrastructure investment
- Operating partner (VP Engineering/Sales): Visibility into their own team's attrition risk, manager effectiveness data for coaching

**Qualification signals indicating pain severity:**
- Unplanned departures (surprise resignations, not planned exits) = acute pain
- Growth targets 30%+ YoY = planning urgency signal
- Board-level compensation audit or ESG initiative = pay equity pain is real
- Recent manager turnover or skip-level feedback showing quality variance = manager effectiveness pain is acute

## For Positioning

**Competitive landscape:**
- Visier: Premium, established, expensive, slow implementation
- Workday analytics: Perceived as "included" but limited predictive capability
- Point solutions: Radford (compensation), Visier (attrition), separate BI tools for headcount
- DIY: Tableau/Looker models built by data teams (labor-intensive, limited scope)

**Win/lose signals:**
- Win when: Workday deployed, CHRO wants unified view of attrition + compensation + planning
- Lose when: Customer believes Workday analytics are sufficient, or has already committed to multi-point-solution approach
- Lose to: Visier (if budget is available and customer wants enterprise vendor)

**Differentiation angle for this segment:**
- All-in-one (attrition + compensation + planning) vs. multiple tools = faster time-to-value
- Built for rapid-growth dynamics (headcount doubling, team scaling, culture retention) vs. steady-state organizations
- Workday-native integration (not a separate platform) vs. bolt-on analytics layer

**Adjacent categories:**
- HR analytics, workforce intelligence, talent management, people data platform (all accurate; positioning should lean into "growth intelligence" for this segment)

## For Messaging

**Buyer language patterns observed:**
- From website: "Turn your people data into business decisions" — speaks to data enablement, not just reporting
- Headcount planning and attrition risk listed first = these are primary value drivers
- DEI mentioned but secondary (don't lead with DEI for high-growth segment; lead with growth + retention)

**Content themes that resonate with this segment:**
- Cost of unplanned attrition (replacement costs, revenue impact from understaffing)
- Scaling without chaos (headcount doubling without losing culture/momentum)
- Manager effectiveness as a lever for retention (coaching high-impact managers, identifying at-risk teams)
- Compensation competitiveness (staying competitive during rapid hiring)

**Buyer motivation framing:**
- They care about: Growth + stability, keeping talent during chaos, making data-informed scaling decisions
- Don't emphasize: DEI compliance (secondary), manual reporting efficiency (nice-to-have), historical analytics (not forward-looking)

**Tone and voice signals:**
- High-growth tech culture: professional but fast-paced, data-driven, outcome-focused
- Speak to growth hunger (they want to scale) + anxiety about talent (they fear losing people)
- Language: "predictive," "ahead of the curve," "intelligent growth" vs. "efficient reporting"

## For Sales Enablement

**Call opening hooks (first 30 seconds):**
- "Most tech companies at your stage see 15-20% unplanned attrition in year 2. We help you predict who's at risk before they leave."
- "Headcount planning becomes a guess after you triple in size. Let me show you how to turn that into a model."
- "You've got Workday, but I'm guessing you're pulling data into spreadsheets to answer basic questions about headcount and pay equity."

**Disqualification questions (to ask early):**
- "How much have you grown in headcount in the past 18 months?" (Below 20% = likely low urgency)
- "What HRIS are you on?" (Not Workday/enterprise = wrong buyer or wrong infrastructure)
- "Have you had any surprise departures in the past 12 months?" (No = low pain, deprioritize)

**Deal structuring note:**
- Standard path: CHRO champion + CFO approval + 4-6 week security + Workday integration validation + board sign-off
- Sales cycle: 5-7 months typical; can be faster if recent funding event + CHRO mandate
- POC: Standard in 70% of deals; validates data quality and integration before full commitment

**Budget conversation framing:**
- Frame against: Cost of replacement hiring (tech companies know this is $200K+/person including lost productivity)
- Position ACV ($120K-$300K) as 2-6 months of replacement hiring costs = ROI is easy if you prevent 1-2 departures
