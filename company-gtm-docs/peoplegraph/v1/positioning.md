# PeopleGraph — Positioning

## Our Vision
We believe every organization deserves clarity into its most important asset — its people. PeopleGraph exists to transform how companies understand, plan for, and invest in their workforce by bringing together fragmented people data into one intelligent platform.

## What We Do
PeopleGraph is a people analytics platform that unifies workforce data from HRIS, recruiting, compensation, and BI systems. It provides predictive attrition modeling, headcount planning, compensation benchmarking, DEI metrics tracking, and manager effectiveness measurement — all from a single integrated data layer.

Unlike Visier, PeopleGraph offers a more accessible entry point for mid-market and growth-stage enterprises without requiring a premium-tier commitment. Unlike Workday's built-in analytics, PeopleGraph works across multiple HRIS platforms and provides specialized predictive models that go beyond native reporting. Unlike point solutions like Radford or PayScale, PeopleGraph combines compensation benchmarking with attrition prediction, DEI tracking, and headcount planning in a single platform.

## Key Differentiators
- **All-in-one people analytics:** Attrition prediction, compensation benchmarking, DEI reporting, and headcount planning unified in one platform — eliminating the need to stitch together point solutions
- **Predictive, not just descriptive:** Built-in machine learning models that identify at-risk employees before they resign, moving HR from reactive to proactive
- **Works with your existing stack:** Integrates across Workday, BambooHR, ADP, and other HRIS platforms rather than requiring a single-vendor ecosystem
- **Faster time to insight:** Pre-built analytics models and dashboards reduce the manual data assembly that bogs down people analytics teams

## Positioning Statement
"PeopleGraph is a people analytics platform that helps HR leaders and people teams make smarter workforce decisions through unified data, predictive insights, and comprehensive benchmarking — all in one place."
