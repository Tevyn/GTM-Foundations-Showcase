# GTM Foundations — Shared Context

## Company Overview
PeopleGraph is a people analytics platform that unifies workforce data from HRIS, recruiting, compensation, and BI systems. It provides predictive capabilities for attrition risk, headcount planning, compensation benchmarking, DEI metrics, and manager effectiveness measurement.

## ICP Context (added by ICP skill)

### Pain Patterns (for PSF to quantify and map to solutions)
**Pain areas identified in ICP work:**
- Manual workforce reporting (data assembly from multiple systems)
- Attrition prediction and visibility into at-risk employees
- Compensation benchmarking and pay equity gap identification
- DEI metrics tracking and reporting
- Headcount planning and forecasting across departments
- Manager effectiveness measurement and its connection to retention

**Buyer outcomes by role:**
- CHRO/VP of People: Strategic visibility, compliance/DEI narrative, cost control, reduced unplanned attrition
- People Analytics Lead: Faster reporting, less manual work, ability to model scenarios
- HR Operations: Implementation support, change management with managers

**Initial problem framing observations:**
- Pain points span operational (reporting) and strategic (planning, attrition) levels
- Current workarounds rely on manual data aggregation across Workday/BambooHR/ADP
- No single existing tool unifies all these capabilities in the target accounts' stacks

### Competitive & Market Context (for Positioning)
**Competitive landscape:**
- Visier is the established people analytics player (premium positioning)
- Workday has built-in analytics (customers may assume this is sufficient)
- DIY/spreadsheet approach common in mid-market
- Some competition from compensation tools (e.g., Radford, PayScale) for benchmarking

**Win/lose signals observed:**
- Win when: organizations have multiple HR systems and need a unified layer
- Lose when: customers believe Workday analytics are sufficient or prefer point solutions
- Differentiation opportunity: DEI + attrition + compensation all in one platform vs. multiple tools

**Adjacent categories:**
- Could be positioned as HR analytics, workforce intelligence, talent management analytics, or people data platform

### Buyer Language & Framing (for Messaging)
**Buyer language patterns:**
- From website: "Turn your people data into business decisions" — positions it as data enablement, not just reporting
- Headcount planning and attrition risk are mentioned first in product features (suggests these are high-value)
- DEI mentioned but in smaller text — may be secondary driver in some segments

**Content themes that map to buyer needs:**
- Cost of unplanned attrition and turnover risk
- Scaling challenges (headcount growth) and planning complexity
- Competitive pressures on compensation (talent retention)
- DEI as compliance/values narrative

**Tone observed:**
- Enterprise-professional (not startup casual)
- Data-driven, analytical, business-focused

## PSF Context (added by PSF skill)

### Problem-Solution Observations
- **Flagship area:** Attrition prediction and proactive at-risk identification. Sales team has a concrete use case here (predictive model + manager intervention workflow). This shows the strongest solution mapping — the product has a clear capability that maps to a recognized workflow pain.
- **Secondary areas:** Compensation benchmarking and pay equity analysis. These are strong capabilities but the pain description stays at category level ("lack of unified view"). DEI reporting is the thinnest — restates the product capability ("consolidate data to create dashboards").
- **Cross-cutting theme:** Fragmentation across HRIS, payroll, and recruiting systems. The core value is unification; all three use cases depend on solving the data aggregation problem first.
- **Buyer language pattern:** "Strategic visibility" and "data-driven decisions" resonates with CHRO/VP buyers. Managers care more about concrete signals they can act on (attrition risk, compensation context). Analytics leads emphasize time savings and scenario modeling.
- **Solution differentiation (early observation):** PeopleGraph's value isn't just reporting — it's predictive capability (attrition models) + competitive benchmarking + DEI in one platform. DIY/spreadsheet workflows can't match the predictive layer; Workday built-in analytics lack the benchmarking and specialized attrition model.

## Positioning Observations (added by Positioning skill)
- **Primary competitors:** Visier (premium people analytics), Workday built-in analytics (sufficient-for-some), DIY spreadsheets (mid-market default), point solutions like Radford/PayScale (compensation-only)
- **Win pattern:** Organizations with multiple HRIS platforms needing a unified analytics layer — PeopleGraph's cross-system aggregation is the entry point
- **Lose pattern:** Workday-only shops that believe native analytics are sufficient, or organizations not yet ready for predictive capabilities
- **Rough positioning claim:** "All-in-one people analytics platform" — combines attrition prediction, compensation benchmarking, DEI, and headcount planning where competitors offer only pieces
- **Perceived differentiators:** Predictive attrition modeling (strongest), multi-HRIS integration, breadth of analytics in one platform
- **Market category assumption:** People analytics / workforce intelligence — category is recognized but fragmented between HR suites, point solutions, and DIY approaches
- **Positioning gap:** No clear value wedge articulated — differentiators are listed as features rather than anchored in a specific buyer's evaluation criteria

## Messaging Observations (added by Messaging skill)
- **Elevator pitch:** "PeopleGraph is a people analytics platform that unifies your workforce data and delivers predictive insights — from attrition risk to compensation benchmarking to headcount planning"
- **Lead value prop:** Attrition prediction (strongest, most polished — "AI-powered attrition prediction that identifies at-risk employees before they resign")
- **Secondary value props:** Unified analytics across HR systems, compensation benchmarking/pay equity, headcount planning and scenario modeling
- **Brand voice adjectives:** Innovative, Trusted, Empowering
- **Messaging pattern:** Feature-framed value props describing product capabilities; no narrative arc connecting them; no buyer language integration; marketing-audience only
