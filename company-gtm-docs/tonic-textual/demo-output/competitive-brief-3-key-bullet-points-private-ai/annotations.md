# Annotations: Give me a competitive brief with 3 key bullet points of how to for a prospect comparing us vs. competitor Private AI

---

> [!v1-passage]
> Private AI focuses on PII detection and redaction, but lacks Tonic Textual's synthesis capabilities. Our platform doesn't just mask or delete sensitive data — it replaces PII/PHI with realistic synthetic alternatives (fake names, phone numbers, medical codes) that preserve semantic context and data structure.

> [!v2-passage]
> Private AI detects and redacts PII/PHI — but it's still fundamentally a redaction tool. When your prospect's data science team fine-tunes clinical LLMs or trains prediction models on redacted notes, they'll hit the same wall: blank tokens destroy the semantic signals models need.

**Section:** Competitive Positioning

**V1:** The original brief lists synthesis as a feature advantage without explaining why redaction actually fails in practice.

**V2:** The focused brief explains exactly what breaks when prospects use redaction, giving reps a hypothesis to test on discovery calls.

**Impact:** Reps who can diagnose the prospect's existing pain before pitching close faster and face fewer stalled evaluations mid-pipeline.

> Healthcare-Specialized NER vs. General-Purpose Detection. Private AI's models are trained across industries — financial services, legal, general enterprise. Textual's NER is trained on millions of real clinical notes across all healthcare specialties. In clinical data, PHI hides in places generic models miss.

**Section:** Differentiation Strategy

**V1:** The original brief leads with audio as a differentiator, a capability that may not match the prospect's actual buying criteria.

**V2:** The focused brief reframes the competition around detection quality in the prospect's own data, inviting a head-to-head proof of concept.

**Impact:** Leading with a testable claim on the prospect's own data accelerates deal velocity by converting feature debates into evidence-based evaluations.
