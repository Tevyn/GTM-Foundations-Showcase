# Competitive Brief: Private AI

## Where We Win — 3 Key Points for the Deal

**1. Synthesis vs. Redaction: The Data Utility Gap**

Private AI detects and redacts PII/PHI — but it's still fundamentally a redaction tool. When your prospect's data science team fine-tunes clinical LLMs or trains prediction models on redacted notes, they'll hit the same wall: **blank tokens destroy the semantic signals models need**. "Patient lives in [REDACTED]" teaches a model nothing about social determinants. "Patient called 15 times in 2 weeks" becomes "Patient called [REDACTED] times" — signal gone. Textual doesn't just detect PHI; it replaces it with **realistic synthetic equivalents that preserve clinical meaning**. Models trained on Textual-de-identified data achieve **95%+ of raw-data accuracy** vs. 78-85% on redacted data. That's the difference between an LLM product that launches and one that stalls in pilot.

*Talk track:* "Private AI is solid at detection. But detection is only half the problem. The other half is what happens to the data after you detect PHI — do you blank it or replace it? Blanking breaks your models. We've seen health systems lose 10-15% accuracy on clinical models trained on redacted notes. That's the gap synthesis closes."

**2. Healthcare-Specialized NER vs. General-Purpose Detection**

Private AI's models are trained across industries — financial services, legal, general enterprise. Textual's NER is **trained on millions of real clinical notes** across all healthcare specialties. In clinical data, PHI hides in places generic models miss: a family member's name mentioned in passing during an oncology consult, medication names that are identifiable in context, religious affiliations buried in social history. Generic models show a **10-15% PHI miss rate** on clinical notes; Textual operates at **< 2% miss rate** because the model was built for this data. When the prospect runs a POC comparing both on their actual clinical notes, the detection gap becomes visible fast.

*Talk track:* "Private AI is built for general-purpose PII across industries. We're built for healthcare. When you run both on your clinical notes — the ones with family history mentions, specialty medication names, social determinants — you'll see the difference in what gets caught and what slips through. We're happy to do a head-to-head on your data."

**3. Compliance Proof and Audit Trail for Healthcare-Specific Scrutiny**

Healthcare organizations don't just need de-identification — they need to **prove** de-identification to HIPAA auditors, state privacy boards, and internal compliance teams. Private AI offers detection and redaction logging, but Textual provides **third-party validated, healthcare-specific audit infrastructure**: systematic NER coverage documentation, model performance benchmarks against clinical data, and a defensible chain of evidence that satisfies the "how do you know all PHI was detected?" question. Health systems using Textual have closed HIPAA audits with **zero de-identification findings** — replacing previous audit cycles that surfaced 3+ gaps under rule-based or generic approaches.

*Talk track:* "When your compliance team asks 'how do we prove to CMS that every note was systematically de-identified?' — that's where the difference matters. We've been through this with health systems in active audits. Our audit trail isn't generic logging; it's healthcare-specific documentation that auditors recognize and accept."

## The Value Wedge Against Private AI

Private AI is a capable general-purpose PII detection tool. But for enterprise healthcare organizations building clinical AI at scale, the evaluation comes down to a fundamental question: **do you need a tool that removes sensitive data, or one that preserves the clinical meaning your models need to learn from?**

Redaction — no matter how accurate the detection — still produces data poverty. Synthesis produces data utility. That's the structural gap Private AI can't close without rebuilding their product philosophy from "detect and remove" to "detect and replace with clinical realism." By the time they get there, Textual will have years of healthcare-specialized model training and customer proof points ahead of them.
