# Annotations: Write a cold outbound email to a buyer in the ICP

---

> [!v1-passage]
> I wanted to reach out because we work with a lot of companies in healthcare, financial services, and insurance who are scaling AI and ML initiatives. A common challenge they're facing: the datasets that would most improve model performance are also the most sensitive — and compliance constraints prevent direct use.

> [!v2-passage]
> I noticed [Health System] is hiring for clinical AI roles and building out LLM workflows — documentation, decision support, claims. That's where the highest-value use cases are.

**Section:** Email Opening

**V1:** The original ICP spans multiple industries and buyer types, so the email opens with a generic industry observation that could apply to anyone.

**V2:** The focused ICP is specific enough that the email can open with a signal the prospect recognizes from their own world — a hiring pattern that reflects their current priorities.

**Impact:** Decision-makers engage with cold outreach only when it reflects their company and context — generic messages get filtered before they reach a conversation.

---

> [!v1-passage]
> Are you dealing with this? Teams often have access to high-quality data like clinical notes, customer conversations, or financial records, but manual redaction approaches break the data apart. They end up choosing between data that's safe but unusable, or data that's useful but risky.

> [!v2-passage]
> Here's the pattern across 200+ health systems: when data science teams fine-tune on redacted notes, accuracy drops 10-15%. "Patient is a smoker from rural Ohio whose mother has hypertension" becomes "Patient is a [REDACTED] from [REDACTED] whose mother has [REDACTED]." The model learns nothing.

**Section:** Problem Framing

**V1:** The original problem-solution work describes pain at headline level, so the email asks whether the prospect has the problem instead of showing it.

**V2:** The focused problem-solution work maps how the pain actually plays out in the buyer's workflow, so the email can describe the mechanism and assert the problem as fact.

**Impact:** Buyers form opinions before talking to sales — content that reflects their actual situation earns consideration, content that describes symptoms they've heard from every vendor gets ignored.

---

> [!v1-passage]
> Tonic Textual is a platform that detects PII and PHI in unstructured data — text, audio, documents — and de-identifies it while preserving context. Instead of crude redaction, we replace sensitive information with realistic synthetic alternatives. Our proprietary NER models are tuned for healthcare, finance, and insurance domains.

> [!v2-passage]
> The obvious move is better rules or building your own NER. But 8 months in, you've tied up a senior engineer on maintenance and the model still underperforms — you're treating the symptom, not the root cause.

**Section:** Competitive Positioning

**V1:** The original positioning leads with product capabilities, so the email reads like a feature list any vendor in the category could send.

**V2:** The focused positioning explains why the typical approach falls short, so the email dismisses the alternative as a peer observation rather than pitching a product.

**Impact:** Feature-first outreach invites comparison shopping and lengthens sales cycles — framing why the current approach fails positions you as the only conversation worth having.
