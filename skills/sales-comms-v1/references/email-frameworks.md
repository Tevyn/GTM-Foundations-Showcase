## 1. Pre-writing checklist

Before writing any sales email, answer these four questions:

**Who** — target segment / persona
- Contact's role and responsibilities
- What they care about
- How the value prop changes for this audience

**Why** — reason for outreach + why this target is right
- Why now? (trigger, timing, market shift)

**What** — differentiator, feature, or pain point to highlight
- Problem / pain point to solve
- Opportunities
- Mistakes to avoid

**How** — how the solution solves the "what" for this audience
- Offers for them
- How an existing similar customer got results
- The path to success story
- Relevant platform features

## 2. Cold email structure (5 components)

1. **Show relevance**: industry insight, common ground, or referral
2. **Transition with a question**: bridge from relevance to your value
3. **What you do**: crisp explanation, no jargon
4. **What it means for the prospect**: translate to their world
5. **Call-to-action**: ask for interest, not time

## 3. Subject lines

The goal is to get the prospect to open the email.

**Effective techniques:**
- Personalization with custom fields (name, company, title)
- Urgency
- Keywords / pain points relevant to the buyer
- Timeliness (seasonality, role change, funding round)

**Avoid:** numbers/metrics in subject, marketing jargon

## 4. Email body best practices

Every sales email must include:
- The reason for your email
- A pain point, benefit, or opportunity
- Call to action

The email content should appear as though you wrote it directly to them. Write like a real human. Avoid jargon and marketing speak.

## 5. CTA design

**Core principle: Balance give and get.** The prospect is giving their time — what do they receive in exchange?

- Frame the CTA based on the benefit to the prospect
- Ask for interest, rather than time
- One clear CTA per email (never multiple competing asks)

## 6. Outbound prospecting strategy

Identify your outbound strategy first, then align the value you offer to that strategy.

**Referral strategy example:**
"I recently worked with Joe at ABC Company, and he recommended I reach out because he thought this was relevant for you as well. Are you using the same provider ABC Company was using previously? I ask because we help companies automate some of the manual functions people in your role are often managing. That was the case for Joe as well, and he's seen X & Y results since moving. I'd love to show you what we implemented with Joe to see if it's relevant for you — would you be interested in seeing one of the workflows my team worked with them on?"

**Personalization hierarchy:** Most personalized messaging goes to your best guess at the decision maker OR highest title in the account.

**Tailoring questions:**
- What similarities does this contact/account have to existing customers?
- What personal priorities might they have in their role?
- Based on research, what recommendations might you have for them?

## 7. Sequence design & cadence

**Rules of thumb:**
- ~10 touch points (mix of email, call, LinkedIn)
- 21-30 day span
- Start with an email to reference in the first call
- Pair calls with other tasks
- 2-3 days between each touch

**Example cadence:**
Day 1: Email + Call | Day 3: Email | Day 4: Call | Day 6: LinkedIn + Call | etc.

**Multiple contacts in an account:** Either stagger timing (if using same messaging) or create persona-specific sequences.

## 8. Follow-up / reply emails

Follow-ups must build on each other and create additional reasons to engage. Never "did you see my last email?" or "checking in."

**Strategies for building relevance:**
- **Considerations/Objections**: Get ahead of potential objections, especially relevancy or urgency
- **Brand Awareness/Trust**: Share customer achievements, social proof, recent customer insights
- **Timeline**: Create urgency with "why now" or time-sensitive offers
- **Competition**: If you can identify their current vendor, ask what they like about them (positive framing)

## 9. Re-engage email strategy

**Who to re-engage:**
- BDR-set appointment no-shows
- MQL not worked
- Wrong contact / no authority unconverted leads
- Dead opportunities (timeline, product feature gaps with updates)
- New contact / department

**Key principles:**
- Check if contact title/role has changed
- Provide compelling "why now" — what's changed since last engagement
- Tie relevance to insights from last engagement
- Must be compelling enough to generate interest even if they don't remember you
- If they DO remember you, generic outreach kills interest fast
- The prospect gives time — what do they receive in exchange?

## 10. Break-up emails

Leave the door open. Ask for a referral. Leave a final value prop.

**Example (numbered response technique):**
"I know you're busy. Consider this the easiest message you'll receive all day.
1. Let's talk, I'm interested.
2. Interested but we have another solution in place. Reach out in 3 months.
3. I'm the wrong person. I'll CC you.
4. Awesome persistence trying to get ahold of me, but no thank you.

Just respond with the appropriate number. I'll take it from there."

## 11. Annotated examples

### Example: Market Force (Cold Email)
**Subject:** "When marketing to New Businesses, the freshness of your data really matters!"

**What works:**
- Image and formatting helps readability
- Value prop easy to identify: "Not able to act on our data as quickly as we compile it? The ROI Network is the answer!"

**What doesn't:**
- Too long
- Multiple / unclear CTAs (call us, visit contact page, forward to right person)
- Too much jargon

### Example: Sendoso (Cold Email)
**Subject:** "Why did the coffee file a police report?"

**What works:**
- Catchy subject that incites an open
- Lighter, playful tone appropriate for the audience
- Formatting makes it skimmable
- Incentive for response highlights their value prop

**What doesn't:**
- Case study link could have more context (metrics/results in the email itself)
- Not personalized enough to the buyer's specific situation
- Marketing speak / jargon
- No urgency

### Example: Lessonly (Re-Engage)
**Subject:** "Lessonly?"

**What works:**
- Timely, clear "why" for reaching back out
- Referenced the last conversation

**What doesn't:**
- Missing a driver or opportunity to pick up the conversation
- Would benefit from more context from past conversations
