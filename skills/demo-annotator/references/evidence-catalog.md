# Evidence Catalog

Curated metrics and citations for supporting annotation Impact statements.

**Catalog policy:** These lines are **starting points**, not literal copy. Select the closest match for the specific Impact you're supporting and tighten it per the Evidence framing rules in `SKILL.md`:
- Lead with the number
- Drop softeners and tactical clauses ("A/B tests show," "experiments find")
- Aim for ~12 words
- If no catalog line is a strong match, substitute an equivalent stat from a credible named source (e.g., *"According to a 2025 [source] report, [finding]."*)
- If nothing fits, leave Evidence blank — a forced match is worse than none

**Tags on each entry:**
- `[proven: <framing>]` — already validated against an Impact framing the Sortly hand-edits established. Preferentially use these.
- `[speculative]` — candidate stat that hasn't yet been used against a known framing. Available, but lower priority.

## Engagement & Conversion

- 40–60% of deals are lost to customer indecision, not to competitors — quantified cost of inaction reduces "no decision" outcomes. `[proven: a brief built around buyer priorities creates urgency]`
- Buying groups that align on the problem — not just the solution — are 2.5x more likely to close (Gartner). `[proven: buyers move faster when they see their problem named precisely]`
- A 2025 Wynter report found 12% of buyers report longer sales cycles with larger buying committees. `[proven: buying-group alignment around the problem]`
- Clear, specific value propositions are associated with 2.3x higher lead-to-customer conversion rates in B2B. `[proven: specific buyer-relevant messaging converts]`
- Moving from feature-first to problem-first language tends to shift conversion from low single-digit to mid-to-high single-digit (2–3x) when underlying fit is real. `[proven: messaging that centers the problem converts]`
- Reports indicate that outcome-specific proof can lift email conversion by 31–104% over generic value claims. `[proven: outcome-specific proof in the buyer's world]`
- One CRO case study describes going from 1.2% to 4.7% conversion after focusing copy on customer outcomes and proof rather than "what we do." `[speculative]`
- Buyers form opinions before talking to sales — early-stage content relevance is critical to earning the next conversation. `[speculative]`

## Retention

- Companies with consistent brand presentation across all platforms see 23–33% higher revenue vs. inconsistent brands. `[proven: a shifting value story makes the product feel interchangeable]`
- When positioning reflects adoption and retention drivers consistently across the lifecycle, early churn drops and LTV increases. `[speculative]`
- Consistency between what is promised in marketing and what is reinforced in sales and onboarding shows up as higher close rates, stronger retention, and more predictable expansion revenue. `[speculative]`
- ICP-aligned customers churn less, adopt more, and expand more — NRR above 120% is the benchmark for well-aligned customer bases. `[speculative]`
