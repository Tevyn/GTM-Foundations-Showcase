# Competitive Brief: Sortly vs. InFlow, Spreadsheets & Other Inventory Tools

## Market Context

Mid-market companies (100-500 employees) across industries like construction, healthcare, warehousing, IT/facilities, and manufacturing are increasingly looking to move off spreadsheets and manual tracking processes. Many are evaluating dedicated inventory management platforms for the first time, while others are looking to replace a basic tool they've outgrown. The competitive landscape includes spreadsheets (Excel, Google Sheets), InFlow, Asset Panda, Inventory Now, and various niche tools.

## Where Competitors Win

**InFlow:**
- Stronger manufacturing and purchase order workflows
- More advanced reporting for complex supply chain operations
- Better fit for companies that need deep inventory management features beyond basic tracking

**Spreadsheets:**
- Free (no license cost)
- Familiar to everyone -- no onboarding friction
- Fully customizable structure

**Asset Panda / Others:**
- Some offer deeper asset lifecycle management
- May have industry-specific compliance features
- Enterprise-grade audit trails in some cases

## Talk Track: When the Buyer Brings Up a Competitor

**If they mention InFlow:**
"InFlow is a solid tool -- where we hear customers run into issues is around complexity. InFlow is built more for manufacturing and supply chain workflows, which means there's a lot of functionality that mid-market teams don't need and don't want to pay for. Sortly is purpose-built for simplicity. Our photo-based tracking and mobile-first design mean your team can be up and running in minutes without any training. We also offer offline access for field teams, which is a big deal for companies with people out on job sites."

**If they mention spreadsheets:**
"A lot of companies start with spreadsheets, and they work fine up to a point. But once you're managing inventory across multiple locations with a growing team, spreadsheets break down -- no real-time sync, no mobile access, no alerts. Sortly gives you all of that without the complexity of enterprise software. And with our bulk import feature, you can bring your existing spreadsheet data right into Sortly in minutes."

**If they mention Asset Panda or other tools:**
"Asset Panda is more focused on enterprise asset lifecycle management. If you need deep compliance tracking or complex depreciation workflows, that might be the right fit. But for teams that need straightforward inventory and asset tracking with great mobile access and a visual interface, Sortly is simpler to deploy and easier for your whole team to adopt."
